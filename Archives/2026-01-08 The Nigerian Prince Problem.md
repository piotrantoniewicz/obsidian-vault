I received a fundraising text today claiming I had said “NO” to a $20,000 tax refund and urging me to click a link, answer a question, and send money to unlock it. This was a couple days after I received a similar message telling me my $20,000 tax refund was at risk because I hadn’t responded. According to the sender, he or she had been trying to call me all day!

![](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:1400/format:webp/1*UdjC53sg5dn1QfJByFxCbw.png)

If this sounds familiar, it should.

This is the Nigerian Prince scam for American politics.

The original scam goes like this. A stranger emails you claiming there is a large sum of money waiting for you and them. The money is supposedly real, already exists, and only requires a small action on your part to be released. A bank account. A processing fee. A little trust.

These texts use the exact same warped psychology.

You already have the money. It is yours. You just need to answer a question and chip in a few dollars to get it.

Different accent. Same scam.

The particular texts I am referencing were sent by a supposed legitimate political organization that has been in existence for years. And this is where the real damage happens.

***(To be clear, these texts WERE NOT sent by any of President Trump’s organizations. These were sent by a mainstream Republican PAC.)***

Republican donors are not stupid. They have spent years being trained by actual scammers, spam filters, and common sense to recognize fraudulent language. Urgent deadlines. Imaginary money. Emotional manipulation. Click now or lose out forever.

When Republican groups use the same tactics, they are not being clever. They are burning trust.

When donors see these fake refund offers, made up matches, or manufactured crisis, many will learn one lesson. These people are willing to lie to get their credit card.

Once that lesson is learned, it does not get unlearned and it impacts us all.

This is why so many Republican donors say they feel used. This is why churn is high. This is why long term value keeps falling. **You cannot build a durable donor base on tactics that look indistinguishable from fraud.**

If we want donors to trust us again, we have to stop treating them like marks.

No more imaginary refunds. No more fake urgency. No more Nigerian Prince fundraising dressed up in red, white, and blue.

Trust is the most valuable asset in life. Once it is gone, no amount of clever copy can buy it back.
