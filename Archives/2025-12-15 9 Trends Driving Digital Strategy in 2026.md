### 1\. AI generated content is everywhere, and most of it is slop

In 2025, AI slop generating tools kept getting better and easier, and now we’re seeing AI slop coming at us from every channel, including major brands like [Coke](https://futurism.com/artificial-intelligence/coke-ai-holiday-ad?utm_source=substack&utm_medium=email), [McDonald’s](https://people.com/mcdonald-s-netherlands-ai-ad-controversy-explained-11867981), and [Disney](https://marketing4ecommerce.net/en/agreement-disney-openai/), political candidates ([Republicans, of course](https://mashable.com/article/gop-trump-ai-deepfakes), but [Democrats too](https://x.com/PeterTwinklage/status/1998953189912240296?s=20)), [spam accounts](https://www.abc.net.au/news/2025-10-27/teens-struggle-to-tell-if-social-media-videos-are-real-or-ai/105924186), [hostile governments](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2025/dec/03/anti-immigrant-material-among-ai-generated-content-getting-billions-of-views-on-tiktok), and more.

Consumers [don’t like AI slop](https://monthindigital.substack.com/p/what-your-ai-content-really-says), but that won’t stop brands and random channels from attempting to numb them into submission.

### 2\. Hyperpersonalization as the new ad unit

Creating custom ads for specific audiences used to be a long, manual process. No longer. Major ad platforms are building tools that allow advertisers to create versions of ads made just for you. This is [the future Zuck promises us](https://www.404media.co/the-future-of-advertising-is-ai-generated-ads-that-are-directly-personalized-to-you/?ref=daily-stories-newsletter): no need for targeting or creative, just tell us what you want to sell.

> “Advertisers are increasingly just going to be able to give us a business objective and give us a credit card or bank account, and have the AI system basically figure out everything else that’s necessary, including generating video or different types of creative that might resonate with different people in personalized ways, and finding the right customers.”

### 3\. Short video is the front page of the internet, and influencers are the infrastructure that powers it.

Short video is the front page of the internet because social and video feeds like TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts are now where huge shares of people, especially young adults, first encounter news and culture, and the time spent in these feeds dwarfs most other formats.

![1 in 5 Americans regularly get news on TikTok, up sharply from ...](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!KOxr!,w_424,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F09157d5f-ef6e-48fd-a586-94805ef41270_640x530.png)

1 in 5 Americans regularly get news on TikTok, up sharply from...

Working with creators is how you get on the front page for the audiences you want to reach. Creators have already done the work of building trust with their followers and know how to reach them. According to a PartnerCentric study, [76% of Americans follow influencers.](https://partnercentric.com/blog/influencer-trust-commerce-statistics/) Brands understand this. [Research from Mordor Intelligence](https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/influencer-marketing-market) estimates the influencer marketing market will grow from about $31 billion in 2025 to over $121 billion by 2030.

### 4\. Everything is TV

As social media becomes less “social,” users are [treating it more like TV,](https://www.derekthompson.org/p/why-everything-became-television?utm_source=substack&publication_id=2880588&post_id=175648601&utm_medium=email&utm_content=share&utm_campaign=email-share&triggerShare=true&isFreemail=true&r=3lep&triedRedirect=true) and the apps are noticing. Instagram and other platforms [are scrambling to launch smart TV apps](https://fortune.com/2025/07/31/tiktok-instagram-tv-apps/) so you can veg out on endless Reels from your couch.

> Today, only a fraction of time spent on Meta’s services—7% on Instagram, 17% on Facebook—involves consuming content from online “friends” (“friend sharing”). A majority of time spent on both apps is watching videos, increasingly short-form videos that are “unconnected”—i.e., not from a friend or followed account—and recommended by AI-powered algorithms Meta developed as a direct competitive response to TikTok’s rise, which stalled Meta’s growth.

On a smaller front, even creators are [building their own broadcast channels](https://newsroom.tiktok.com/introducing-bulletin-board?lang=en) using in app features to push videos directly to their followers without having to worry about followers talking back.

### 5\. Social platforms turning into full shopping malls

With everything becoming TV, it makes sense that home shopping is back, this time as a decentralized network.

Look at TikTok Shop. Its U.S. sales hit [more than $500 million over just Black Friday](https://www.businessinsider.com/tiktok-shop-crossed-500-million-in-us-sales-black-friday-2025-12) to Cyber Monday this year, and [social commerce more broadly](https://www.sellerscommerce.com/blog/social-commerce-statistics/) is on track to cross $100 billion in the U.S. by 2026. People are on their apps and primed to buy.

### 6\. Fractured Platforms: YouTube, Facebook, & Instagram are only networks reaching 50%+ Americans

In one of the year’s best films, *[Eddington](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oL6jZqExlIk)*, a small town struggles to deal with the alternate realities its residents inhabit, despite being neighbors, because of their media habits.

![A line chart showing that TikTok, Instagram, WhatsApp and Reddit have continued to gain users in recent years](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!yG_l!,w_424,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F01c3dddc-0914-43c5-8c33-8ad758bf9583_840x1040.png)

A line chart showing that TikTok, Instagram, WhatsApp and Reddit have continued to gain users in recent years

Even though majorities use the same three major social networks, our feeds are increasingly personalized, meaning everyone is experiencing their own version of the internet. The “one main character” internet of the 2010s has been replaced by endless main characters across niches and corners of the web.

> “During the rise of Facebook as a kind of universal platform, and now this feels Neolithic*,* there were these moments of really universal instant global culture,” said Ben Smith, the co-founder and editor in chief of Semafor.
> 
> With the [rise of algorithmically driven social media platforms](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/10/09/style/end-of-viral-internet-tiktok-instagram.html?utm_source=substack&utm_medium=email) like TikTok, moments that get people around the world talking, on cable news and at the dinner table, are becoming less common. Each user’s feed is hypertailored to them, meaning no one sees the same version of the internet.

What this means in practice is that you need to be on the platforms where your audiences are most active and partner with creators who already have relationships with them.

### 7\. Owned audiences, newsletters, and private spaces as insurance

One positive trend is that while people are becoming less social on legacy social media apps, [they are creating smaller, more private](https://www.searchenginejournal.com/the-rise-of-micro-communities/532677/), and more exclusive spaces to talk to each other.

You can see this in the [growth of Reddit,](https://investor.redditinc.com/news-events/news-releases/news-details/2025/Reddit-Announces-Third-Quarter-2025-Results/) a site I hope can figure out how to keep the AI slop flood at bay, Substack, the return of message board style features across social apps, and even the revival of DMs on YouTube. Everyone seems to be in a good group chat now, from the Secretary of Defense to my high school best friends.

### 8\. Gens Z & Alpha as the Household’s Chief Media Officers

Young people now curate much of their [families’ screen time](https://www.gwi.com/press-releases/gwi-kids-new-dataset) and [purchase decisions](https://www.marketingdive.com/news/gen-alpha-holds-significant-sway-over-parents-purchases/742922/). They shape what parents watch, which apps households adopt, and how brands, news, & politics enter the home. Influence no longer flows top down through mass media. It moves sideways and upward, mediated by the youngest people in the room.

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!iZg8!,w_424,c_limit,f_webp,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff8a2dd81-f0a9-431b-be9b-f6c571068a90_1404x662.png)

If your media plan talks about “adults 25 to 54” but ignores the 16 year old controlling the kitchen table’s For You Page, you’re missing where culture and commerce begin.

### 9\. Social Slows Down

As platforms decentralize and algorithms and users make feeds more personal, social media is slowing down. The strongest creators are no longer chasing trends. They are focused on producing quality content that resonates with their audiences.

> This is a theme I’ll keep digging into — it’s not a one-post problem — but across all these conversations, [the message is clear: depth over breadth in 2026.](https://liahaberman.substack.com/p/icymi-were-heading-into-our-slow)
> 
> - Post less, but make the posts count.
> - Experiment with long-form content that holds people’s attention.
> - Create spaces — especially offline spaces — to connect with the people who love your brand.
> - Embrace printed materials, tactile retail experiences, and ritual-based packaging as an antidote to digital burnout.
> 
> We’re not logging off completely — we’re just showing up more intentionally.

This shift includes the rise of microdramas, produced by brands, creators, [foreign production companies,](https://digiday.com/marketing/in-graphic-detail-the-rise-of-micro-dramas-that-are-attracting-big-ad-dollars/) and [even Hollywood.](https://www.wsj.com/business/media/gammatime-microdrama-video-platform-aa333984)

So take a breath. Plan more. Get creative. Partner and co create with influencers. Don’t feel the need to chase every trend. Focus instead on connection and audience engagement.
