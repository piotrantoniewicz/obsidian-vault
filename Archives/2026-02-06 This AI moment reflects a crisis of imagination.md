The Davos promenade was lined with Silicon Valley tech companies taking over chocolatiers and fine cutlery shops. All had billboards with vague platitudes to AI: ROI for AI. Security for AI. AI for AI. AI was everywhere, and yet marginal in its vision. Are we really claiming that all AI has to offer is streamlined CRMs and HR agents?

I was struck by the crisis of imagination.

In my world, the tech nonprofit ecosystem is thinking big. To name just a few, tech and AI-powered nonprofits are using AI to [discover new drugs to cure cancer](https://www.rebootrx.org/), [offer every teacher real-time coaching when they need it](https://learningequality.org/kolibri/about-kolibri/), and [feed people in local communities](https://www.foodhelpline.org/).

I believe *these* are the public interest use cases that will create an AI revolution worth investing in. One that benefits humanity. This is the opportunity for AI for humanity.

**The Hope**

To be fair, there were spaces at Davos that were different.

In every room he was in, [Demis Hassabis](https://www.linkedin.com/in/demishassabis/) from Google DeepMind painted a picture that was downright soulful. He wrestles with the very essence of what it means to be human — what of humanity must be preserved, and what limitations of humanness could (and should) be unlocked. He didn’t pretend to know how to answer these questions. He invited artists, policy makers, priests, and human rights activists to dine. It was comforting. It sparked a genuine dialogue.

At an event to discuss AI for Global Health, [Jamie Drummond](https://www.linkedin.com/in/jamiedrummond/) and [David McNair](https://www.linkedin.com/in/davidmcnair/) ’s ONE Campaign blew me away. With the erasure of global datasets, it’s hard to visualize just how drastically aid cuts are impacting children. The ONE Campaign circulates the data that’s been erased, making it sociable and actionable. Decades of work now live in one place, harnessed by AI. It’s a cross-sector tool for practitioners, activists, and journalists alike. Unilateral decisions cannot undo good work as long as good people still care.

This is the opportunity for AI for humanity. The stories of AI-powered nonprofits are inspiring. Creativity, ingenuity, and progress. Public interest tech spurs revolution.

**The Rupture**

This is the AI race that the United States should care about most. Our country’s legacy is one of creativity, ingenuity, and progress. Yet the USA House at Davos was in a centuries old church, which had been covered in Bald Eagles and signage for our 250th anniversary. It felt like a billboard off of I95 — like a tired version of the past, not a hopeful vision for the future.

This is despite the fact that the vast majority of the promenade was overtaken by American brands, American media companies, and Americans. The U.S. fell in the [2026 Global Soft Power Index](https://static.brandirectory.com/reports/brand-finance-soft-power-index-2026-digital.pdf), measuring country brand awareness. Meanwhile, South Korea rose thanks in part to KPop. The main message I took from this? Creativity cultivates power. Also, Demon Hunters rule.

In line at the airport coming home from Davos, a Berliner asked me if I thought this was the end of the American Empire. Honestly, I said yes. But I also said that the magic of America is that it reinvents itself over and over and over again. This is the opportunity for AI for humanity.

**The Opportunity**

The week after Davos, I flew home to San Francisco for Fast Forward’s Alumni Retreat. It’s the best week of the year. Not only did I get to hang out with my favorite people — our tech nonprofit founders — but I felt a restored sense of optimism.

Throughout the four days, we got as philosophical as we did technical. These leaders are wrestling with big questions: How can AI help reach more people? What does it look like to scale responsibly? How do I make the miracle visible to funders who don’t understand the potential of AI?

Mozilla CTO [Raffi Krikorian](https://www.linkedin.com/in/rkrikorian/) imparted a decisive message: “Live your values and push them out.” AI is widening the aperture around *how* we push out our values — using technology for good.

- [Adalat AI](https://www.linkedin.com/company/adalat-in/) created a speech-to-text transcription software to address the stenographer shortage in India, tackling court backlogs and delivering fair and timely trials. Adalat already works with courts in 9 Indian states, and their team is growing their engineering team quickly to expand into other countries.
- [Visilant](https://www.linkedin.com/company/visilant/) built accessible AI-powered eye screenings to eradicate avoidable blindness in low-resource settings where ophthalmologists are rare. Visilant has already screened 30,000 patients and expects to reach more than one million within three years.
- [ASAP](https://www.linkedin.com/company/asap-together/) is a legal tech nonprofit supporting immigrants as they navigate the U.S. asylum system. In 2025, the organization’s AI-powered membership service helped secure five-year work permits for more than 3.4M asylum seekers, unlocking greater stability and opportunity for families nationwide.

This is just the start. 12 years ago, I co-founded Fast Forward to empower nonprofits building tech and AI for humanity. When we started, the term “tech nonprofit” didn’t even exist. Now there are over 1,000 tech and AI-powered nonprofits around the world.

I challenge others who walked the promenade at Davos to dream bigger. Embrace AI for how it can benefit humanity.
