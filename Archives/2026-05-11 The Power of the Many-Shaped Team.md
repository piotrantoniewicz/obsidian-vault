### TEAMS! Newsletter | Teams Edition | May 11, 2026

The most effective teams in the social impact sector rarely resemble traditional organizational charts. Systemic change requires a "team of teams," a radical collaboration among federal, state, and local agencies, private lenders, architects, and community advocates. By analyzing the successful opening of **Mitchell Park Place** in Palo Alto, this edition of TEAMS! explores how diverse stakeholders can align around a singular "North Star" to solve one of the Bay Area's most "unsolvable" challenges: affordable housing and inclusive living for neurodiverse community members.

These large challenges aren’t lacking vision; it’s the friction of fragmentation that makes them so hard to solve. As nonprofit and social impact leaders, we often feel like we’re shouting into a canyon, trying to bridge the gap between ambitious and aspirational goals and the labyrinth of bureaucratic, financial, and community hurdles. We see the need vividly, but the path to execution is often blocked by siloed thinking and "not in my backyard" resistance.

If you have ever felt your mission is too big for your organization alone, you are right. The era of the "Lone Ranger" leader is over. Today’s victories belong to those who curate and steward a complex ecosystem of partners. Mitchell Park Place, a 50-unit affordable housing development, is a blueprint for what happens when we stop protecting our silos and start building bridges, expanding our definition of what a team can be.

### Key Takeaways: The Anatomy of a High-Impact Team

- ***Visionary Stewardship:*** Leadership begins with a singular "North Star" vision that compels others to join.
- ***Radical Inter-Agency Collaboration and Community Support:*** Success in complex projects requires seamless integration of federal, state, county, and city resources, along with community support.
- ***The Hybrid Funding Model:*** Mission-driven housing projects are fueled by a "stack" of public grants, private commercial lending, and tax credit investments.
- ***Inclusive Design & Advocacy:*** A team is incomplete without the voices of those it serves, and it integrates community advocates and future residents from day one.
- ***Operational Agility:*** Navigating bureaucracy requires a commitment to "record-breaking" speed and to ministerial support.

### Visionary Stewardship

Every great movement starts with a catalyst. At Mitchell Park Place, **former Santa Clara County Supervisor Joe Simitian** provided the initial vision and the relentless drive to launch the project. In the nonprofit world, "visionary" is often a hollow buzzword, but true visionary leadership is about *stewardship,* holding the space for a project for years until the right "team of teams" can be assembled. It is about moving a project from a conceptual dream to a county priority, and Joe Simitian did exactly that.

### The "Team of Teams": Radical Collaboration

Mitchell Park Place succeeded because it broke through the "silo" trap. It took more than a dedicated organization, like **Eden Housing**. It was a coordinated effort involving:

- **County of Santa Clara:** Providing the land and more than $12 million in funding.
- **City of Palo Alto:** Providing funding through its Local Housing Trust Fund and achieving approval in a record-breaking four months.
- **State & Federal Agencies:** Including the California Tax Credit Allocation Committee and HUD, which provided the structural financial backbone. When your team includes the Lieutenant Governor, Congressmen, and City Managers, you aren't just running a nonprofit; you are leading a coalition.

### Hybrid Funding

In today’s economy, no single check covers a project of this scale, estimated at more than $30 million. The "team" must include the financial sector. Mitchell Park Place secured significant private investment from Wells Fargo Bank through tax credits, along with a $5 million permanent loan from Century Housing Corp. This illustrates a critical lesson for all leaders: your "team" must include commercial partners who view social impact as a viable investment, not merely a charitable donation.

### Design with, Not For

The true brilliance of this project lies in its unique focus: **25 units reserved for the Intellectual and Developmental Disability (“I/DD”) community**. This wasn’t a happy accident; it was the result of a partnership with **AbilityPath** and **Housing Choices Coalition**, alongside architects at **Architects FORA**, who integrated community input into the very soul of the building. When we include service providers and residents such as **Ty Kawaguchi** and **Gabi Dedek** in the "team," we ensure the final project truly serves the people it was built for.

### Speed as a Mission Value

Nonprofits are often bogged down by the "slow grind" of approvals and sign-offs. However, the Mitchell Park Place team proved that excellence and speed can coexist. The City of Palo Alto’s four-month approval record is a testament to what happens when "City Staff" (from the City Manager to Senior Planners) see themselves as part of the mission team rather than merely the regulatory gatekeepers.

### Putting It into Action: 5 Steps for Leaders This Week

- ***Identify Your "Visionary Champion":*** Who is the champion in your network (Board, Government, or Corporate) with the vision to anchor your next big move? Engage them this week.
- ***Be Creative With Your "Funding Stack":*** Look beyond traditional grants. Are there tax-credit opportunities or community lending partners, such as Wells Fargo, you haven't approached?
- ***Invite a "Service Partner" to the Table:*** If you are a private business building a product or service, invite an organization such as AbilityPath to provide "operational insight" before finalizing the design, so that it better meets the needs of all community members.
- ***Slash a Bureaucratic Barrier:*** Identify one internal process that slows your team down. Challenge your "City Manager equivalent" to find a "ministerial approval" path for it. Align them with the mission and community benefit.
- ***Listen to the "End User":*** Schedule a 15-minute call with someone like Ty or Gabi, the actual beneficiaries of your work. Their perspective is the ultimate reality check.

### The Close

As we celebrate the residents who now call Mitchell Park Place home, we are reminded that our work is ultimately about people. Families and individuals are finding independence and community today because a "many-shaped team" decided that "impossible" was just a starting point. Let us lead with that same audacity, knowing that when we align our North Stars, we can accomplish amazing things together.
