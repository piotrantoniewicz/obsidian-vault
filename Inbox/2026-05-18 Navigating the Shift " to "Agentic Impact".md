### TEAMS! Newsletter | AI Edition | May 13, 2026

You’ve survived the great "AI Noise" of the headlines of 2024 and the experimental pilots of 2025, and you may be asking, “What’s coming next?” Today, you are likely facing the new, more profound transformation. The novelty of "chatting with a bot" has faded, replaced by an urgent operational reality: the gap between organizations that use AI as a high-tech typewriter and those that use it as a strategic impact multiplier is widening.

You feel it in your board meetings when directors ask about "algorithmic stewardship" rather than just "overhead costs." You feel it in your fundraising: major donors now expect "AI-assisted workflows" to mitigate the overhead burden and burnout that have historically plagued our sector. Soon, you will no longer be managing just a technology solution; you will likely be managing a hybrid workforce of humans and digital "interns." This edition of the TEAMS! Newsletter is designed to help you navigate this maturing and evolving landscape, not as a tech enthusiast, but as a nonprofit and community-based organization.

### Executive Summary: The 2026 Agentic Thesis

In 2026, AI in the nonprofit sector is no longer a "technical initiative" managed by IT; it is a driver of organization-wide transformation, affecting all corners of the organization. This edition focuses on the transition from **Generative AI** (content creation) to **Agentic AI** (workflow execution). By mastering the "AI Intern" mindset and following a phased implementation roadmap— *Crawl, Walk, Run, and Fly* —organizations can reclaim up to 20% of their team's capacity, enabling greater direct program impact. This requires shifting from simple prompts to complex, role-based directives that prioritize transparency, accountability, and the "Human-in-the-Loop" standard.

### Key Takeaways

- ***The "AI Intern"****:* Shift your culture to view AI as a 24/7 assistant that requires precise "onboarding" (prompting) to become an effective “intern”.
- ***Prompting as a Core Organizational Competency****:* Effective organizations in 2026 are expert AI-prompt "context-setters," using Role, Context, Task, and Format to drive high-quality digital outputs.
- ***"Human-in-the-Loop" Governance****:* As AI moves closer to decision-making, radical transparency with staff, donors, and stakeholders regarding AI usage is the new baseline for stewardship.
- ***Phased Implementation Over "Big Bang" Rollouts****:* Success comes from starting with simple administrative "friction points" before scaling to specialized applications for program and client-facing support.
- ***Platform Specialization (Tool-Persona Alignment)****:* Organizations will want to match specific organizational needs, such as research, tone-heavy communication, or data synthesis, to the distinct "personalities" of different AI LLM models.

### The 2026 AI Framework

**From "Tools" to "Digital Colleagues":** The days of viewing ChatGPT or Claude as simple website queries are over. In 2026, we are now viewing them as **LLM-based colleagues**. These models have been trained on vast amounts of data to do more than just search for information. They are now capable of "connecting the dots" across disparate data points to generate new, customized answers.

- ***Real-World Scenario:*** A development manager uses a "Role" prompt to turn their AI into a veteran Development Director. Instead of asking it to "write an email," the DSP asks the AI to "review our previous three years of gala appeals (attached) and identify which emotional hooks resonated most with our $1,000+ donor tier.” The AI doesn't just write; it analyzes and strategizes.

**Mastering the Art of "Digital Directives":** Prompting has evolved from a "cheat sheet" of hacks into a structured form of communication. To achieve better results and save time, managers must provide high-context instructions. The 2026 standard follows four steps: Give a **Role**, establish **Context**, outline the **Task**, and specify the **Format**.

- ***Real-World Scenario:*** An Executive Director facing a complex regulatory change uses a "Consult with an Expert" prompt. They tell the AI: "Act as a nonprofit compliance attorney \[Role\]. I am reviewing the new 2026 state-level workforce mandates \[Context\]. Summarize the top 5 risks for a mid-sized agency \[Task\] and present them in a table with 'Risk Level' and 'Mitigation Strategy' columns \[Format\].”

**Solving the "Friction of Care":** The true "North Star" of AI in social services is the **Automation of repetitive administrative tasks**. When we use AI to draft meeting notes, summarize calls, or handle basic data entry, while we’re being more "efficient", we are, more importantly, freeing our staff to focus on higher-value, impactful work.

- ***Real-World Scenario:*** Caseworkers at a vocational center spend 30% of their week on session summaries. By using a "Summarize" prompt on their voice-to-text notes, they reduce that time to 5%. This "recovered time" is reinvested immediately in 1-on-1 job coaching, directly improving participant outcomes.

**The Strategic Value of "AI Personalities":** In 2026, "one-size-fits-all" AI is a myth. Different platforms have developed distinct strengths, or "personalities."

- **Claude** is the choice for **authentic tone** in donor emails and sensitive staff communications.
- **Gemini** is the powerhouse for **research and recommendations**, leveraging its integration with live data and Google's ecosystem.
- **Perplexity** has become the gold standard for **fact-checking and citations**, essential for evidence-based grant writing.
- **Microsoft Copilot** acts as the internal "workhorse," living inside Word and Teams to summarize internal operations.
- **NotebookLM** has emerged as the “sense-making engine” for complex information, transforming documents, meeting notes, and research into synthesized insights, study guides, and conversational knowledge bases.
- **ChatGPT** serves as a versatile strategic partner, excelling in brainstorming, writing, analysis, and workflow acceleration across everything from board communications to operational problem-solving.

**Stewardship: Transparency and the Human Guardrail:** As we lean into AI's around-the-clock availability, we must prioritize transparency **and Accountability**. Donors in 2026 are savvy; they want to know that AI is supporting your mission, not diluting your humanity. This means disclosing AI usage and ensuring that a "Human-in-the-Loop" reviews every piece of AI-generated content before it reaches a participant or a funder.

- ***Real-World Scenario:*** During a crisis, a leader uses AI to generate an immediate response draft. However, the leader spends 20 minutes refining that draft to ensure the organization's "voice" remains present and that the facts are 100% verified. This is the essence of AI-assisted, human-led leadership.

**Putting AI to Work: Five Practical Steps for This Week**

- ***Onboard Your "Intern"****:* Choose one tool (Claude, ChatGPT, or Gemini) and use a "Role" prompt for a task you’ve been procrastinating on, like a first draft of a board report.
- ***Find and Eliminate Friction****:* Ask your department heads to identify one "robotic" task (such as summarizing weekly check-ins) that can be delegated to an AI assistant.
- ***Build a Prompt Library****:* Create a shared document where your "tech champions" can save their most successful prompts, such as "Draft a donor thank-you note that emphasizes community impact.”
- ***Practice the 80/20 Rule****:* Use a prompt to learn a new skill or a complex topic: "Give me the most important 20% of information about \[X Topic\] that will help me understand 80% of it".
- ***Draft Your Transparency Statement****:* Write a concise internal memo for your board and staff that outlines how the organization uses AI responsibly to maximize your primary services.

### Closing Thoughts

The transition we are witnessing in 2026 is not about technology replacing people; it is about technology making people more "available." In the social services sector, our greatest asset has always been our presence, our ability to sit with a family in crisis, coach a participant through a challenge, and dream of a more inclusive world. By using AI to absorb the administrative "noise," we reclaim that presence. We move at the speed of need, powered by our mission's heart.
