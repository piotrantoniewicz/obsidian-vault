### International Mission Board

#### Experiment Summary

Timeframe: 01/20/2026 - 02/03/2026

Each year, IMB runs a Calendar acquisition offer that consistently performs well. The campaign follows a standard funnel: users land on a page to request the calendar, then are redirected to a donation page to complete their gift.

This year, we noticed a new addition to the landing page—a small form disclaimer that was not present last year. Although subtle, the change raised concerns about whether it might be creating friction and negatively impacting conversion.

To evaluate its effect, we ran an A/B test over a two-week period, splitting traffic evenly between the version with the disclaimer and the version without it.

#### Research Question

We believe that removing the form disclaimer for 2026 Calendar landing page visitors will increase sign-up rates by eliminating unnecessary friction and reducing distractions, thereby preventing users from being drawn away from the form and allowing them to focus solely on completing their request.

#### Flux Metrics Affected

The Flux Metrics analyze the three primary metrics that affect revenue (traffic, conversion rate, and average gift). This experiment produced the following results:

0% increase in traffic  
× 10.1% increase in conversion rate  
× 0% increase in average gift  

#### Key Learnings

The key learning from this experiment is that removing the form disclaimer from the landing page for the 2026 Calendar acquisition offer led to a 10.1% increase in Email Acquisition for All Traffic. This change was statistically significant at a 100.0% confidence level, indicating a clear impact on user behavior. Moving forward, it's important to remember that reducing unnecessary friction and distractions can improve conversion rates. By simplifying the user experience and allowing visitors to focus solely on completing their request, we can potentially see higher engagement and sign-up rates. In future experimentation, it may be beneficial to continue testing different elements on the landing page to further optimize the conversion process. This could include testing different forms of messaging, images, layouts, or additional features to see what resonates best with users. By consistently running experiments and analyzing results, we can continue to improve the effectiveness of our campaigns and drive better outcomes for our nonprofit.
