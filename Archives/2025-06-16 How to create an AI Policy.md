The third and final webinar in our series looking at how AI can support the third sector’s data use to find out how to create an AI policy or guidelines for your organisation.  
  
Many organisations, especially smaller ones, have no policies for putting tools in place, let alone using them ethically. Understand the most important principles from existing AI policies and other organisations prototyping generative AI tools.  
  
Your speakers  
Dulcie Vousden is DataKind UK’s Head of Data Science, where she has spent the last five years supporting third sector organisations to use their data more effectively and advocating for the ethical and responsible use of data in the sector. Prior to DataKind UK, she completed a PhD in medical biophysics and has over 15 years of experience designing and analysing data-focussed projects.  
  
Kirstin Williamson is the AI Product Manager at Wellcome Trust, where she leads the development of strategies and initiatives to leverage AI for operational efficiency, innovation and responsible use. Her work focusses on the safe and effective integration of AI technologies across the organisation, including the creation of policies, governance frameworks, and a growing centre of excellence.  
  
The organisers  
DataKind UK is a small charity that has spent over a decade helping the UK's charities, voluntary organisations, community interest companies, social enterprises, and more to achieve their missions and use data science to become more impactful.  
  
The Insight Infrastructure programme, developed by the Joseph Rowntree Foundation, aims to democratise access to high-quality quantitative and qualitative data and evidence through open collaboration and innovation, to help tackle injustice and inequality in the UK.
