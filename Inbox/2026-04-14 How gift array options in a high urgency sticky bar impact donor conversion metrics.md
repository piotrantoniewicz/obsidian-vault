International Mission Board

Experiment ID: #201859

### International Mission Board

#### Experiment Summary

Timeframe: 01/15/2026 - 01/19/2026

During IMB’s 2026 Winter Olympics campaign, we re-tested the sticky bar’s gift array buttons to understand their impact on donor behavior.

In reviewing the original execution, we identified opportunities to improve how the sticky bar guided visitors toward giving—particularly around campaign value proposition and giving array amounts. We re-tested this approach to determine whether modifying the sticky bar could more effectively encourage gifts and to gather data needed to inform future fundraising tactics.

For this test, 50% of site traffic was shown the standard sticky bar experience featuring a single call-to-action button. The remaining 50% received a treatment version with three buttons mirroring the suggested gift amounts on the donation page, allowing visitors to engage with a giving option earlier in their journey.

#### Research Question

We believe that adding a gift array to the HUC sticky bar for IMB website visitors will increase engagement and donor conversion by minimizing the number of steps on the donation page, reducing friction and decision-making, resulting in more gifts.

#### Key Learnings

This experiment shows that adding a gift array to the IMB website’s sticky bar significantly increased both visitor engagement and donor conversion. The treatment version—featuring three suggested gift amount buttons—outperformed the standard sticky bar by 448.4% in Clicks for All Traffic, with 99.9% statistical confidence.

These results suggest that introducing giving options earlier in the donor journey reduces friction and simplifies decision-making on the donation page—leading to more completed gifts.

**Downstream Impact on Donations:**

To understand how increased engagement translated into giving behavior, we analyzed downstream donation metrics. During the test period, the control version generated 1 gift, while the treatment generated 16 gifts, representing a 1,495% increase in transactions (100% LOC).

This confirms that the gift array not only drives more clicks but also meaningfully motivates visitors to complete a donation.

**Gift Amount Performance:**

We also evaluated which gift array buttons led to the most donations:

- $25 Button: 6 gifts, all at $25
- $50 Button: 3 gifts, all at $50
- $100 Button: 3 gifts (2 at $100, 1 at $150)
- More Options: 4 gifts, resulting in varied amounts ($20, $50, $50, $200)

Notably, most donors retained the amount they selected in the sticky bar through to the completion of the donation, reinforcing the value of early commitment.

**Conclusion & Recommendation:**

Overall, the gift array on the sticky bar effectively removes a key decision point from the donation page, leading to higher engagement and more completed gifts.

For future testing, IMB should continue exploring gift arrays and similar friction-reducing elements in high-visibility areas of the site. Ongoing iteration will help further optimize the giving experience and maximize donor conversion and revenue.
