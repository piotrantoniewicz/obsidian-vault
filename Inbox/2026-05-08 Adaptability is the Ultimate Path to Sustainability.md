### TEAMS! Newsletter | Sustainability Edition | May 8, 2026

A decade ago, "work" meant the four walls of a shared office, a physical whiteboard in the breakroom, and a full team working side by side. Those days are gone. Today, we are no longer bound by traditional office hours, locations, or even skill requirements. We’re experiencing a radical compression of time and expectations that affects everyone on our team, from the front-line case manager to the leadership team. In this new reality, the ability to adapt and pivot is the only true form of job security.

Leaders are expected to deliver ever-greater impact, yet many remain locked into 20th-century organizational structures even as the ground beneath their feet has shifted, including how talent works, how technology scales, and how careers are built. The "tried and true" playbook for retention and productivity no longer works; the script has been entirely rewritten. For those of us in the social impact sector, this is a fundamental shift in how we steward our missions and our people toward a sustainable future.

### Executive Summary

The traditional career "ladder" has evolved into a "jungle gym" where adaptability is the only true form of job security. This shift is moving future roles toward a **skills-first, AI-integrated, and flexibility-driven** operational model. To thrive, organizations need to prioritize internal mobility and continuous learning to retain team members who now value "how" they work as much as "what" they do.

### Key Takeaways

- ***The Power of the Focus***: Agile teams are making a greater impact in far less time than it once took. They can quickly assess and adjust programs to meet rapidly evolving community needs.
- ***Flexibility as a Retention Engine****:* Although remote roles account for only 9% of job listings, they attract 40% of all applications. Where possible, consider hybrid models essential for retaining talent.
- ***The Skills-First Revolution****:* Leaders are increasingly looking beyond resumes to value "curiosity and heart," recognizing that skills matter as much as formal education.
- ***The Era of Constant Recalibration****:* Employees are on track to hold twice as many jobs as previous generations, prompting a shift from linear ladders to "jungle gym" careers. Given this reality, organizations need to understand how to operate effectively in it.
- ***AI as a Skillset Reshaper****:* Roughly 85% of professionals will see at least 25% of their core skills transformed by AI, necessitating continuous learning.

### Doing More in Less Time:

Today, technology enables a team to deliver reports and billing in minutes by automating processes that once took hours or even days. This shift has raised expectations and compressed timelines across every department.

- ***Nonprofit Scenario:*** Imagine a regional social services agency that traditionally requires a full-time intake team to process community applications over a month. By adopting AI-driven data synthesis, a smaller, focused team can now process those applications in a week, freeing staff to provide more direct, face-to-face support to families in crisis.

### The Geography of Talent: Winning the Local War for Attention

In early 2020, only one in 67 job postings offered remote work. Today, remote and hybrid roles account for 60% of all application interest. Even within a single region, workers have tasted flexibility and are reluctant to give it up. Research shows that workers with the ability to work remotely are more engaged and more likely to be happier in their positions.

- ***Nonprofit Scenario:*** A regional provider struggles to find a specialized compliance officer within a 20-mile radius. By shifting the role to a hybrid model that allows deep-focus work at home three days a week, they attract a top-tier local candidate who was previously deterred by a grueling daily commute, ensuring the agency gets "best-in-class" talent rather than "best-in-proximity."

### Dismantling the Degree Barrier

Only 40% of professionals and one-third of executives still believe a four-year degree is essential to a successful career. Companies that adopt skills-first hiring are twice as likely to place talent effectively and 98% more likely to retain high performers. In the direct care service sector, recognizing this reality can empower organizations to hire often-overlooked talent they might otherwise miss.

- ***Nonprofit Scenario:*** A youth mentorship organization decides to stop requiring a bachelor's degree for its Program Coordinator role. Instead, it hires a local resident with five years of lived experience and a record of successful community organizing. This hire brings a level of trust and "heart" that a degree cannot provide, resulting in deeper community engagement.

### Cultivating the "Boomerang" Culture

Retention today looks different. Some of the best-performing companies are seeing nearly twice as many "boomerang" employees (those who leave and later return) as other companies. This is because these organizations invest in internal mobility and their people's growth, creating a "pull" that endures even after employees leave.

- ***Nonprofit Scenario:*** A case manager leaves an agency to start a local community garden project, a common trend as more professionals add "founder" to their profiles. Because the agency supported their growth and fostered a strong culture of stewardship, the worker returned two years later as a Program Director, bringing entrepreneurial skills to help expand the agency’s services.

### AI Literacy as the New Standard

AI literacy is the fastest-growing skill on the market, with 100% year-over-year growth. It is not just for tech companies; at least a quarter of the skills for most of the workforce could be reshaped by AI. Organizations that provide structured learning see their teams develop these vital skills 3 to 4 times faster.

- ***Nonprofit Scenario:*** A local legal aid nonprofit integrates AI training for its paralegals. Instead of spending 20 hours a week on manual case research, the team learns to use AI-assisted tools, enabling them to take on significantly more pro bono cases without increasing staff burnout.

### Your Playbook to Putting It Into Action:

- ***Update Job Requirements****:* Review one open job description this week. Remove the "College Degree Required" line and replace it with a list of specific, demonstrable skills and qualities, such as "curiosity" and "adaptability".
- ***The "Internal First" Challenge****:* Review your internal talent pipeline. Over 40% of jobs at top-performing organizations are filled internally; identify one staff member for a "stretch assignment" today.
- ***The Flexibility Audit****:* Evaluate your current hybrid policy. Since 60% of applicants seek these roles, identify which positions can transition to a "standards and trust" model rather than an "attendance" model.
- ***Re-engage a "Boomeranger"****:* Reach out to a high-performing former employee. Since many successful companies see double the return rate among employees, a quick check-in could secure your next senior leader.
- ***Invest in AI Literacy****:* Dedicate one hour during your next staff meeting to an AI "learning sprint." Teams that invest in learning develop these critical skills 3 to 4 times faster.

### The Close

The mission of social impact is too important to be slowed by outdated notions of "how work should look." The organizations that will lead the next decade are those that view their people as adaptable explorers, ready to lead into the future. Your role as a leader is no longer just to “manage”; it is to steward a culture of constant growth and pivot-readiness. When you invest in your team's adaptability, you future-proof your mission.
