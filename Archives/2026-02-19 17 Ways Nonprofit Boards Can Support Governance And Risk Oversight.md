As a nonprofit grows, so too does the complexity of running it. Expanding programs, shifting funding streams and rising community needs can stretch teams thin and make it harder to keep the mission at the center of every decision. In that environment, a board’s role isn’t just ceremonial. Strong boards help clarify who is accountable for what, weigh in on strategy, and pinpoint potential pitfalls before they become crises.

Governance, long-term planning and risk oversight all sit squarely within a nonprofit board’s remit, yet how that plays out can vary widely from one organization to another. Below, members of [Forbes Nonprofit Council](https://councils.forbes.com/forbesnonprofitcouncil) share their perspectives on how boards should step up to meet today’s challenges in the nonprofit sector.

## 1\. Maintain Mission Focus

Today’s challenges make every issue seem imminent and urgent. Boards play a critical role in helping nonprofits remain focused on their mission. With so much change, nonprofits must balance immediate needs with the implications of taking on new focus areas. When boards support leadership in looking past the current crisis, it can make a difference in an organization’s long-term sustainability. - [Taryn Palumbo](https://councils.forbes.com/u/f1ed38a9-ec63-4105-baa7-e1d81493b882), [Orange County Grantmakers](https://ocgrantmakers.org/)

## 2\. Act As Enterprise Stewards

Engaged boards are essential to helping nonprofits navigate complexity. Boards must move beyond a narrow fiduciary role to act as true enterprise stewards. For instance, boards shouldn’t just approve plans—they should help shape them. Their value is in bringing external perspective, asking the right questions and helping leadership think long-term about impact, risk and sustainability. - [Andrea Levere](https://councils.forbes.com/u/3e4c8200-3789-4f0e-ae6d-f298efdf0345), [Capitalize Good](https://www.capitalizegood.org/)

## 3\. Leverage Diverse Experience And Perspective

It’s crucial to have a board that leverages different types of experiences and perspectives. This enhances how executives view risks and opportunities, especially in a complex environment where no day is the same as the last. And when we give our board real license for feedback and honest input, our leadership team’s decisions become much sharper. - [Anne Marie Dougherty](https://councils.forbes.com/u/db9190fa-861c-4f3d-baff-f96e6610d209), [Bob Woodruff Foundation](https://bobwoodrufffoundation.org/)

## 4\. Serve As A Strategic Partner

The board has a fiduciary responsibility to the organization and its stakeholders. However, they should always act as partners to staff and others and never as a “watchdog.” The board is responsible for the culture of the organization. When there is alignment with the mission, strong commitment and passion, the results will come. - [Magdalena Nowicka Mook](https://councils.forbes.com/u/54767ecd-4f6c-4270-a7d7-46a02d2fa524), [ICF (International Coaching Federation)](https://coachingfederation.org/)

## 5\. Provide Big-Picture Oversight

Nonprofit boards should provide high-level oversight by setting the strategic direction. They should ensure legal and ethical governance and actively monitor financial and operational risks. This involvement is crucial because the board holds the ultimate fiduciary and legal responsibility for the organization, protecting its mission, assets and long-term sustainability. - [Yuriy Boyechko](https://councils.forbes.com/u/8e4d9015-de53-4b1f-a37f-4abb6f31e959), [Hope For Ukraine](https://hfu.org/)

## 6\. Support The CEO With Resources And Counsel

First and foremost, board members must be strong resources for their CEOs. Rather than managing the day-to-day operations of the organization, the job of the board is to ensure the CEO has the resources, the support and the counsel needed to do a more and more complex job. They are partners with the executive director in this and need to respect that role. Otherwise, it becomes adversarial fast. - [Patrick Riccards](https://councils.forbes.com/u/898b58dc-cef6-43bd-a82b-67a754c84b57), [Driving Force Institute](https://drivingforceinstitute.org/)

## 7\. Stay Focused On Strategy

Boards add the most value by staying focused on strategy rather than getting pulled into the weeds. Because nonprofits are often risk-averse by nature, diverse boards help leadership see risk and opportunity more clearly. When board members bring different experiences, challenge assumptions and stay anchored in the mission, they strengthen governance and support better decision-making without micromanaging. - [Karen Cochran](https://councils.forbes.com/u/ef2a7a6a-3a79-476a-b428-5274b0822dbf), [Philanthropy Innovators](https://www.philanthropyinnovators.com/)

## 8\. Set Strategic Guardrails

Most nonprofit boards try to steer from the front, but the smarter ones design better guardrails. In today’s complexity, boards should focus less on setting direction and more on defining boundaries, including values, risk thresholds and unacceptable trade-offs. The best boards do not give the map; instead, they ensure the organization does not fall off a cliff while finding its own path. - [Cherian Koshy](https://councils.forbes.com/u/c7030e74-4bd7-4f84-bec7-9f665a65c1d8), [Kindsight](https://kindsight.io/)

## 9\. Serve As A Strategic Sounding Board

Boards should help leaders sleep at night. Their job is not to run the work but to pressure-test it, spot what might go sideways, and make sure the mission still makes sense when things get messy. Good boards ask smart questions early, stay calm in risky moments, and back leadership with clarity instead of second-guessing after the fact. - [Michael Bellavia](https://councils.forbes.com/u/0ac4f8a0-0fd4-4547-b39b-f10f5d48ad4d), [HelpGood](http://www.helpgood.com/)

## 10\. Clarify The Vision And Provide Resources For The Mission

Strategically, boards help define the vision and mission to ensure that plans align with long-term objectives. They are responsible not just for approving strategic plans, but also for ensuring the organization has the necessary resources to implement those plans, especially when it comes to staffing. By actively engaging, boards help nonprofits remain mission-focused, resilient and accountable to stakeholders. - [Victoria Burkhart](https://councils.forbes.com/u/f6d331b9-6f5a-4314-b8b6-e4ccc169316c), [The More Than Giving Company](https://www.morethangiving.co/)

## 11\. Identify And Share Responsibility For Risk

The best boards respond to complexity by thinking critically, asking questions and becoming a solution partner. They should think about scenarios that the staff might be too busy to see and ask questions about ideas baked into the strategy. But most importantly, they also need to share in the solutions. Boards cannot just identify risks; they must also commit to sharing in the work to solve or mitigate them. - [Matthew Gayer](https://councils.forbes.com/u/63c2351d-6601-4eff-baf8-d9b5bbd450a3), [Spur Local](https://www.spurlocal.org/)

## 12\. Function As A Unified Team

Boards should function like a hand, not individual fingers, with each role working together on governance, strategy and risk oversight. When responsibilities are balanced and shared, no single member carries undue pressure. This collective approach reduces stress, minimizes friction and strengthens organizational resilience and decision-making. - [Jamee Rodgers](https://councils.forbes.com/u/99e80e99-c9ac-44c6-9fe0-49b6685455bd), [Community Foundation of Rappahannock River Region](https://www.cfrrr.org/)

## 13\. Align Expertise With Governance Roles

Intentionally match board members to committees that align with their expertise. When the right people steward the right domains, governance strengthens, strategy sharpens and risk oversight becomes more disciplined. Thoughtful alignment enables boards to think collectively and act decisively, delivering leadership that is more coordinated, informed and effective than any single voice. - [Randy Wong](https://councils.forbes.com/u/c9915109-1d18-41bd-b146-29557ab20e8b), [Hawaii Youth Symphony](https://www.hiyouthsymphony.org/)

## 14\. Secure Sustainable Revenue

Boards play a critical role in securing diverse and sustainable revenue, particularly through philanthropy. By leveraging their networks, modeling donor engagement and supporting fundraising strategy, boards reduce overreliance on funding sources and strengthen organizational resilience to support adaptive strategy and effective risk management. This enables organizations to remain credible and financially sustainable. - [Arvetta Jideonwo](https://councils.forbes.com/u/2ee80d1b-ac5b-48f6-a03e-388551f6c7c6), [Wheeler Mission](https://wheelermission.org/)

## 15\. Monitor Regulatory Requirements And Trends

Board members should be aware of new state and federal requirements and monitor trends to determine how they could impact the organization and its mission. One example is how organizational charters are being scrutinized and reviewed. This could put a nonprofit’s status at risk. - [Kimberly Lewis](https://councils.forbes.com/u/de0a52bf-5bba-4e94-9fd3-4b16b2bd95f3), [Goodwill Industries of East Texas, Inc.](https://www.goodwilleasttexas.com/)

## 16\. Integrate AI Into Organizational Strategy

Whether or not we believe in singularity, tech advancements aren’t slowing down. Meanwhile, NGOs struggle with the multiplier effect of tech due to no funding, outdated tools and lengthy processes, yet they are asked for results by funders. Boards must prioritize foundational investment in human capacity and data over pilots. Their role is to ensure AI is part of the mission’s operating system. - [Jacek Siadkowski](https://councils.forbes.com/u/894fd369-b576-4f32-9c0d-6d31e211a9b8), [Tech To The Rescue](https://techtotherescue.org/)

## 17\. Guide Responsible Growth

Boards play a vital role in guiding governance, strategy and risk as nonprofits grow. Trusted board relationships help leaders expand responsibly without losing momentum. When the mission stays at the center, boards provide the insight and oversight needed to make smart, disciplined decisions that protect impact and ensure long-term success. - [Chloe Hoeft](https://councils.forbes.com/u/e9b3bb0d-0fec-4ce1-ae25-71c54f182900), [Venu Arts & Culture Foundation](https://venufoundation.org/)
