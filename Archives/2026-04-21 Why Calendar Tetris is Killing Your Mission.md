### TEAMS! Newsletter | Engagement Edition | April 21, 2026

Check your calendar for tomorrow. If it looks like a game of Tetris, with blocks falling too fast and you’re one "quick sync" away from a total system collapse, you aren't alone. We have entered an era of "Meeting Theater", where we mistake visibility for value. We convince ourselves that if we can see our team’s faces on a 4x4 Zoom grid, they are engaged. We believe a packed schedule is the hallmark of a high-performing executive. By the way, I will admit, I am guilty of this, too.

In reality, most leaders are drowning in the "Meeting Tax." While we are in meetings, our real work, including strategic thinking, donor cultivation, and program innovation, piles up in the shadows. We are exhausting our most valuable resource: our people's time and energy. Engagement isn't a byproduct of attendance; it is the feeling of making a meaningful contribution and connection. If your team is spending 50% of their week talking *about* work, they have only 50% left to actually *do* it. For many, meetings are the sands running out through the hourglass.

### Executive Summary

Shifting from a "Synchronous-Default" culture to an "Async-First" mindset is worth considering as we aim to stay engaged amid demanding schedules. High engagement stems from autonomy and the protection of "Deep Work" sessions. By reviewing our "Meeting Tax," using AI for status updates, and replacing "Mandatory Fun" with authentic digital connection, we treat our team’s time as a sacred resource. This can lead to a more focused, less burned-out, and more mission-aligned organization.

### Key Takeaways

- ***Presence Is Not Performance:*** Just because a staff member is on the call doesn't mean they are engaged.
- ***The 23-Minute Context Penalty:*** Every "quick" interruption costs nearly half an hour of recovery time for focus and strategic thinking.
- ***Meetings are “Luxury Goods”:*** We must stop using a highly valuable tool (everyone’s collective time) for cheap problems (status updates).
- ***Asynchronous Communication Can Be More Inclusive:*** Moving to written or recorded updates allows "Night Owls" and "Early Birds" to contribute at their peak energy.
- ***Trust is the Best Engagement Tool:*** Giving your team time back is the loudest signal that you trust their professional agency.

### The "Meeting Tax" on Creativity

In the nonprofit world, our resources are always lean. We track every cent of our overhead, yet we're not using our time as wisely as we can. Consider "Context Switching." Research shows it takes an average of 23 minutes to refocus after an interruption. When we pepper a marketing manager’s or a grant writer’s day with three "30-minute syncs," we haven't just taken 90 minutes; we have effectively destroyed their day’s potential for more "Deep Work."

For example, imagine a Program Director at a social services agency trying to draft a complex impact report. A "quick question" via email or a mandatory mid-afternoon check-in disrupts the flow. The result? They stay late to finish work they couldn't do during "work hours." This isn't engagement; it’s a recipe for burnout. We must recognize that meetings can be a very “expensive” intervention. If the goal is simply to transfer information, a meeting is probably the least efficient way to do so.

### Building an "Async-Culture”

Engagement happens when people have the autonomy to choose *when to* contribute. Transitioning to an "Async-First" culture doesn't mean we stop talking; it means we stop talking unnecessarily *at the same time*.

***The "Voice-First" Update:***Instead of a 30-minute status sync, encourage your team to use 2-minute voice notes or short video snippets and send via text. This retains the "human" element, the tone, the warmth, the humor that text lacks, but it removes the scheduling headache. It allows a busy manager to listen while commuting and a team member to record when they’ve actually hit a milestone and have a break in their day.

***Collaborative Silence:*** Most organizations, especially **n** onprofits, thrive on collaborative energy, and there is definite value in in-person connection. But today’s realities require additional ways to stay connected. For example, using shared documents allows people to comment at their peak energy times or when their day allows. This levels the playing field for a team that might not speak up in a crowded Zoom room but has brilliant insights when given space to write.

***The Social Watercooler Re-imagined:*** Culture isn't built in "Mandatory Fun" Zoom happy hours. Those often feel like an extension of the workday. So much of culture is built on "side-chatter," the memes, the shared wins celebrated in real time on chat platforms, and informal recognition of a job well done. When we move the "business" to asynchronous channels, the time we spend together in person or on video can be used for what matters: relationship building and complex problem-solving.

***The Shift: AI as a Buffer, Not a Burden:*** As we integrate AI into our workflows, we should use it to protect our time. AI can summarize long email threads or transcribe meetings for those who didn't need to attend. Imagine a world where only two people attend a meeting, and AI generates a high-fidelity summary for the rest of the board. When communicated appropriately, this can tell your team: "I value your focus and time more than your attendance."

### Putting It into Action: Five Practical Steps

- ***The “Calendar Purge”:*** Review your calendar for next week. Choose one recurring "status" meeting and delete it. Replace it with a collaborative thread, a quick call, or even a 2-minute video update.
- ***Declare a "No-Meeting Block":*** I know it’s hard, but try setting aside a time when the entire organization agrees to zero internal meetings. Protect the "Deep Work" of your mission-critical staff.
- ***The "Five-Minute" Rule:*** If a meeting is called, an agenda must be sent 24 hours in advance. If the agenda can be answered in 5 minutes, cancel the meeting.
- ***Check Your "Urgent" Culture:*** Ask your team, "How often do you feel you have to drop everything to respond to a non-emergency message?" Use their feedback to establish "Quiet Hours."
- **Humanize the Async:** Send a "gratitude" voice note to a team member this week. Show them that "Async" doesn't mean "unconnected and out of sight."

### Closing Thoughts

Engagement is about supporting and fueling human energy. When we stop cluttering calendars with "Meeting Theater," we give our teams more breathing room to care about their work again. Respecting time is the ultimate engagement tool. Give your team their time back, and they will give you their best ideas in return.

Let’s stop playing Tetris with our calendars and start building the future of our missions.
