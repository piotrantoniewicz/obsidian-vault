### TEAMS! Newsletter | Engagement Edition | May 12, 2026

The way people access information has undergone a more radical transformation in the past year than in the previous twenty. Search is no longer primarily a list of "ten blue links"; billions of users now receive direct answers from AI assistants such as ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini. This shift means traditional SEO, focused on ranking and clicks, is being replaced by Answer Engine Optimization (“AEO”), where success is measured by being **cited** as a trusted source in an AI-generated response. This edition of TEAMS! It is a more detailed “how-to guide” to help you begin the process of your organization’s visibility.

### The Three Principles of Quality Content

Leading brands like Carta, Ramp, and Webflow have moved past the "slop" era of high-volume filler content to focus on three core pillars:

- ***Information Gain:*** AI models reward novelty. Organizations must publish proprietary data, internal insights, and unique viewpoints that expand a model's knowledge rather than restating common information. For example, Webflow saw a **6x higher conversion rate** from AI-sourced traffic after focusing on original material.
- ***Humans in the Loop:*** While AI can accelerate research, humans must remain in charge of voice, accuracy, and judgment. Modern teams are hiring for "content engineering", a hybrid role blending systems design with quality control.
- ***High-Velocity Updates:*** Freshness is now a primary authority signal. Content updated within the last three months is **three times more likely to be cited**. Teams that refresh content 3x faster dominate both traditional Google results and AI visibility.

### Key Takeaway: The " AEO Search” Framework

This edition of TEAMS! introduces a repeatable four-step process for marketing teams to maintain visibility:

***1\. Prioritize Investment:*** Use data to identify which queries matter most. Teams should focus on "high-impact topics" across their site, external domains, and community threads.

***2\. Precision Creation and Refresh:*** Automate triggers to update content every 60 to 90 days. Use precise AI workflows to ensure content follows structural hierarchies such as sequential (HTML tags: H1-H2-H3) that AI agents need to extract information.

- **HTML Heading Tags**. These are structural elements used to organize content on a webpage by importance and hierarchy.

> Think of them like the outline of a book: the H1 is your title, H2 are your chapters, and H3 are the sub-sections within those chapters.

---

- **Definitions and Hierarchy:**
- ***H1 (Heading Level 1):*** The primary heading of a page. It tells both users and AI agents what the entire page is about. Typically, there is only **one** H1 per page (usually the main title).
- ***H2 (Heading Level 2):*** These act as subheadings that break the main topic into major sections. They provide the main "meat" of the content's structure.
- ***H3 (Heading Level 3):*** These are sub-subheadings used to further divide the information within an H2 section. They provide granular detail.
- **Why "AI Agents" Need Them:** These tags are necessary for AI agents to "extract information." This is crucial for a few reasons:
- **Semantic Mapping:** AI doesn't just "read" text; it looks for relationships between ideas. A sequential hierarchy () tells the AI that the text under an H3 is a specific detail belonging to the broader topic of the H2 above it.
- **Scraping Efficiency:** When AI tools (like LLMs or search crawlers) scan a site, they prioritize headings to create a summary. If the hierarchy is messy (e.g., jumping from an H1 to an H3), the AI might misinterpret the data’s importance or context.
- **Accessibility:** Beyond AI, these tags are vital for screen readers, helping visually impaired users navigate content logically.

---

***3\. Measure New KPIs:*** Success is no longer just impressions and clicks. Teams must track **Brand Visibility** (appearance in AI responses), **Citation Rate** (frequency of use as a source), and **Share of Voice** (visibility relative to competitors).

***4\. System of Record for Trust:***Create a single source of truth for product knowledge, brand voice, and verified data. This prevents "context drift" and ensures every AI-generated output remains factual and on-brand.

### The AEO Power of Structure and Off-Site Validation

Data from 12,000 analyzed pages shows that structural elements significantly boost AI citation odds:

- **FAQs** increase citation likelihood by **40%**.
- **Clear heading hierarchies** increase citation odds **2.8x**.
- **Lists and tables** appear in nearly **80% of ChatGPT citations**, compared to just 29% of top Google results.

Furthermore, **85% of brand mentions** in AI search originate from third-party sources rather than a brand's own website. Brands are **6.5x more likely** to be cited through external validation. Key off-site surfaces include **Reddit** (cited in ~22% of answers) and **YouTube** (crucial for "how-to" queries).

### Organizing for Success

To operationalize these ideas, consider a four-role structure:

- **Context Librarian:** Governs the "brand truth" and positioning.
- **Content Engineer:** Designs the scalable workflows and integrates AI tools.
- **Strategy Lead:** Translates performance data into smart priorities.
- **Executive Sponsor:** Clears obstacles and sets the mandate for speed and experimentation.

### The Compounding Loop

Visibility in 2026 is a compounding loop: original ideas generate content worth refreshing; fresh content earns third-party mentions; and mentions spark community conversations that fuel the next wave of ideas.

> Organizations that treat their content operations as core infrastructure, structured and high velocity, will sustain authority, while those relying on old SEO playbooks will disappear.
