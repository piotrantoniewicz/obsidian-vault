## What Is GivingTuesday?

What Is GivingTuesday?

GivingTuesday 2025 is on December 2 and will be here before you know it.

Doesn’t it seem like the major holiday season begins earlier and earlier every year?

Thankfully, charitable giving rises alongside the holiday spirit. In fact, more charitable donations are made at the end of the year than any other time.

One way to kickstart the season of giving is by participating in [GivingTuesday fundraising](https://www.causevox.com/use-case-giving-days/).

This annual “Global Day of Giving” event takes place on the first Tuesday after Thanksgiving (this year it’s December 2 which might feel late but is sneaking up fast) and has helped to amplify year-end fundraising efforts for nonprofits and charitable causes since 2012.

But the beginnings of this international day of giving were quite humble. Just thirteen years ago, the 92 Street Y in NYC and the United Nations Foundation considered how they could cut through all the noise of Black Friday and bring back the spirit of giving. What started out as a simple idea quickly transformed into what is now [GivingTuesday](https://givingtuesday.org/).

It’s never too early to start planning how your nonprofit or cause can get in on all the energy, excitement, and benefits of the big day.

## 1\. Lay the Groundwork Today for a Powerful GivingTuesday

People don’t just give because they’re asked to. They give because they’re motivated by the story of a friend, a video you post on social media, or the client spotlight in your e-newsletter. Giving is the result of inspiration, empathy, and connection.

So start your GivingTuesday planning by considering who you want to inspire and how you want to do so. Data from [GivingTuesday 2024](https://www.givingtuesday.org/blog/givingtuesday-2024-record-breaking-results/) reveals that there was a 16% increase in giving from 2023. Globally, young people are far more active givers and since donating online is the most common way for people to give on GivingTuesday, especially for the younger generations, it’s a good idea to focus on your online audience. Think about your email lists, your social media followers, and your partner organizations.

### So what does GivingTuesday planning look like, exactly?

The best way to plan this [giving day](https://www.causevox.com/blog/giving-days-how-they-work-how-your-nonprofit-can-participate/) is to think about it as you would any other fundraising campaign. It’s not just a 24-hour event (even if it only “runs” that long). Instead, it’s an opportunity for [storytelling](https://www.causevox.com/blog/the-complete-guide-to-storytelling-for-nonprofits/), [donor cultivation](https://www.causevox.com/blog/donor-cultivation-cycle/), supporter engagement, and a way to build awareness around your organization and mission. Follow these fundraising plan best practices, including:

- Set a goal based on your organization’s needs and using [SMART goal](https://www.causevox.com/blog/smart-fundraising-goal-setting-plan-succeed/) criteria
- Note your go-to strategies for success (such as a particular type of fundraising campaign, ideal length of the campaign, etc.)
- Size-up your resources, including staff support and budget
- Create a fundraising site ([create your free campaign site with CauseVox here](https://www.causevox.com/fundraising-sites-pages/))
- Create a thorough plan that addresses:
	- Campaign audience and the message that will best resonate with them
		- Expenses
		- Goal
		- Any additional items of note

## 3\. Make Your GivingTuesday Campaign Unforgettable

GivingTuesday is a great opportunity to test out new fundraising techniques, messaging, and tools. Since most of the action is centered one day and one campaign, you can try something new without the risk of a long-term commitment.

Over the years, we’ve learned that GivingTuesday draws a notable increase in donations and online engagement, so it’s worth trying something different such as using [CauseVox](https://www.causevox.com/) to launch a [peer-to-peer fundraiser](https://www.causevox.com/blog/peer-to-peer-fundraising/) to expand your donor base and recruit more supporters.

Of course, this doesn’t mean you have to launch a new website the same day or try something so out of the box that you’re setting yourself up for failure. Put all of your ideas on the table and then work with your team to figure out which ones are best for a day like GivingTuesday.

Consider one or more of these different techniques:

- Get a [matching grant](https://www.causevox.com/blog/the-complete-guide-to-matching-grants/) from a partner or major donor. Campaigns with matching grants work extremely well for short timelines. Plus, it’s publicity for both your campaign and the sponsor. Jessica from the Adult Congenital Heart Association found a match made a big difference in her campaign:  
	“Prior to this, like most organizations, we tried to drive GivingTuesday donations through our social media channels, but did not see the momentum we were able to capture with the added match and a single channel to donate,” she says.
- Do something [fun and different on social media](https://www.causevox.com/blog/giving-tuesday-social-media-tips/) such as trying out a new short video trend from Tik Tok, utilizing Instagram stories, making updates on creating a special GivingTuesday hashtag for your campaign, or [going LIVE](https://www.causevox.com/blog/livestreaming-tips-nonprofit-virtual-fundraising/) on Facebook throughout the day.
