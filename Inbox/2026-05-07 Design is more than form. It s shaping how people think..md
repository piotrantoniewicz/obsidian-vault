## The end of independent thinking

Just a few years ago, technology helped us make decisions. Today, it is starting to influence which decisions even come to mind in the first place.

For product designers and teams building AI solutions, this is a huge shift. We are no longer designing only screens, features, and buttons. We are designing the way people perceive the world.

## AI is starting to filter reality

System 0 works like an intermediary between a person and information. It does not just show data. It analyzes the situation, predicts the user’s needs, and provides ready made answers.

In practice, this means we are less and less likely to see the internet as it really is. Instead, we receive a version that has already been filtered by algorithms.

In the past, users compared websites themselves, checked sources, and drew their own conclusions. Today, AI increasingly does this for them. People no longer analyze multiple perspectives. They receive one ready made answer.

That is why System 0 is becoming more than technology. It is slowly becoming part of our everyday life and our way of thinking.

## Convenience has a price

For years, technology companies have tried to make users’ lives easier. Fewer clicks. Faster answers. Less effort.

This worked well in ordinary applications. The problem begins when AI also starts taking away the effort involved in thinking.

If a system constantly searches for information, summarizes it, and tells us what is important, people stop exercising their own minds.

Over time, they may struggle with:

- focusing attention,
- searching for information independently,
- critical thinking,
- analyzing different opinions.

It works a bit like muscles. If we do not use them for a long time, they weaken. Our minds may work in the same way.

Research shows that long term use of AI systems may weaken analytical ability and independent thinking. This is an important signal for people designing products.

Because user convenience does not always mean something good.

## Comfort does not always help us grow

Most AI systems are designed to make things convenient for the user. Algorithms learn our behavior and show us content we are likely to enjoy.

The problem is that people grow when they encounter something new, difficult, or uncomfortable.

Meanwhile, System 0 increasingly removes these kinds of experiences. It shows us similar content, similar opinions, and similar people.

As a result, information bubbles are created. Users mainly see things that confirm their existing views.

The accidental discovery of new topics and perspectives also disappears. In the past, you could come across something completely unexpected on the internet. Today, algorithms try to match everything to our previous behavior.

This may make people less curious about the world and less open to other opinions.

## AI products are starting to control attention

It has long been known that the way information is presented influences people’s decisions. AI does this even more strongly.

AI systems decide:

- what we see first,
- what we probably will not see,
- which information we consider important,
- which answer we consider the best.

This means that designers of AI products are beginning to design the user’s attention. And that is a huge responsibility.

Today, many systems are designed mainly to keep the user’s attention for as long as possible. The problem is that something that increases engagement does not always help a person think better.

## AI is changing the way we acquire knowledge

This is already very visible in search engines.

Research shows that when users receive a ready made summary created by AI, they click on sources much less often and almost never verify them.

Just a few years ago, people read many pages and compared information. Today, they increasingly trust a single answer generated by a system.

This means that product designers have more and more influence over the way users think.

Every decision concerning:

- the order of information,
- the visibility of sources,
- the way an answer is explained,
- the level of content simplification,
- affects how people understand the world.

## The growing power of algorithms

One of the biggest problems is that algorithms are increasingly deciding what is important.

AI chooses which content we see and which information we trust. This gives enormous power to the technology companies that control these systems.

If algorithms can predict our emotions and behavior, they can also influence our decisions.

This is no longer just about shopping. It is also about political views, social opinions, and the way we perceive reality. As a result, different people begin to live in different versions of the internet and different versions of the world.

## Future generations may think differently

The biggest effects may only appear in a few years. Children growing up in a world full of AI may develop completely different thinking habits than previous generations.

If technology simplifies everything and predicts everything for the user from an early age, it may affect:

- creativity,
- concentration,
- patience,
- independent thinking,
- relationships with other people.

That is why designing AI products is no longer just a technological topic. It is becoming a social issue.

## We need more responsible design

The solution is not to reject AI completely. AI systems help us deal with an enormous amount of information.

So the most important question is not “should we use AI?”, but “how do we design AI responsibly?”.

There is increasing discussion about the need to protect the user’s cognitive autonomy.

This means creating systems that do not only provide ready made answers, but also help people think independently.

Such products should:

- show different perspectives,
- encourage users to check sources,
- explain how the algorithm works,
- leave room for reflection.

Transparency is also becoming very important. Users should know when AI is influencing their decisions.

## Will we preserve our independence?

System 0 can greatly help people. It can speed up information analysis and make everyday life easier.

At the same time, it may make people less and less independent. That is why the discussion about AI is no longer only about technology, but about the future of human thinking.
