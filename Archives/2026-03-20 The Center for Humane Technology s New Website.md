**A public-facing platform designed to support one of today’s most essential conversations about our future with technology and society**

The Center for Humane Technology (CHT) has spent years helping society see something that had long been felt but rarely articulated: the way technology’s incentives and design quietly shape our attention, emotions, civic life, and democratic systems. Their work reached a global audience through the Netflix documentary The Social Dilemma, which transformed a diffuse public anxiety into a widely shared understanding of structural technological harm.

As their influence deepened, CHT’s role changed. What began as a catalytic moment grew into an institution working across policy, product, research, and public understanding. Tectonica joined them at this turning point to help build a digital home capable of carrying that expanding role — a site that could serve policymakers and parents, technologists and journalists, donors and organizers, without flattening their differences or simplifying the complexity of the issues.

We’ve been fans of CHT’s work for a long time. Their thinking has shaped and sharpened many of our own ideas over the years, particularly around how incentives, narratives, and profit-based tech design shape collective agency. In many ways, CHT is working at the very same core problem that animates Tectonica’s mission — how to build systems, stories, and structures that strengthen healthy society and democracy rather than erode it — just viewed from a different angle. Where their lens interrogates the technological incentives that shape human behavior, ours examines the political and civic structures that determine how people participate in collective agreement and action. The alignment between these approaches made this collaboration deeply natural and aligned from the outset.

This project became about more than a website: it was about creating the online infrastructure for a movement that must help society confront — and meaningfully shape — the trajectory of artificial intelligence and persuasive technologies.

### From Spark to Institution

CHT emerged from a moment of clarity: an honest naming of how persuasive technologies were misaligned with human flourishing. That clarity generated a wave of public resonance and volunteer energy, eventually culminating in The Social Dilemma. But the organization quickly evolved beyond that moment.

Internally, the work had grown into a multi-disciplinary center of researchers, policy experts, technologists and communicators. Externally, however, the public still largely associated CHT with founding voices and a film. The organization needed to be understood as a place where serious, long-term, system-level work happens — not simply a messenger of warning, but a site of solutions and guidance.

At the same time, the landscape was shifting. Social media harms already required sophisticated explanation; AI multiplies the complexity. AI is more diffuse, harder to localize, and easier to treat as inevitable. CHT wanted to avoid both extremes: the empty reassurance that “everything will work out,” and the despairing sense that nothing can change. The new site needed to communicate that balance.

### The Strategic Challenge

By the time CHT approached us in early 2025, several converging needs had become clear.

- **Leverage and grow even further beyond the powerful voice of their founders** and present the full depth of their team — showing the organization as a center of interdisciplinary expertise.
- **Speak to multiple actors in the system**, each with distinct forms of power: policymakers, philanthropic decision-makers, people inside tech companies, journalists, educators, concerned citizens, and organizers.
- **Explain complex systemic harms**, especially around AI, in ways that felt tangible and accessible without losing intellectual rigor.
- **Create a sense of agency** for people across these roles — giving them language, clarity, and pathways without falling into either naivety or doom.
- **Build something expandable**, capable of supporting future campaigns, rapid-response content, new research, and evolving thought leadership.

Our work together was to translate these needs into a single digital foundation that could hold the weight of CHT’s mission.

### Our Approach: A Collaborative Strategy Process

We began by leading a series of intensive strategy sessions with CHT’s team across research, policy, design, communications, and leadership. These were not perfunctory interviews but deeply engaged conversations that surfaced the tensions the organization navigates daily: the pull between grassroots resonance and grasstops influence; the challenge of staying ahead of the field without becoming illegible; the need to name structural harm while giving people a place to go with that knowledge.

Through this process, several core principles emerged:

- The site must tell a **unified story** that resonates across vastly different roles.
- Complexity must be introduced **gradually**, not all at once.
- The tone must remain **human, grounded, direct, and hopeful**, without false simplicity.
- The platform must feel **institutional and stable**, yet dynamic enough to support rapid response.
- The structure must mirror **CHT’s theory of change**, not a marketing funnel.

We translated the strategic insights into user journeys that recognized the real roles people play in the technological ecosystem — whether drafting legislation, designing products, allocating resources, teaching children, investigating harms, or trying to understand a system that feels increasingly beyond their control.

To explore the range of possible presentations, we developed three contrasting homepage concepts: one centered on institutional clarity, one infused with movement energy, and one attempting to straddle the two. The internal conversation they generated helped solidify a clear direction: a site anchored in the structure and credibility of a center, with enough warmth and hope to express an invitation to participate.

### Transforming Strategy into Structure

One of the central structural decisions was to organize the site around core **areas of work and future-oriented themes**, rather than around audience categories or internal departments.

Visitors can enter through issues such as “AI in Society” or “Social Media in Society”. Each theme acts as a narrative gateway into deeper content: a clear description of the problem, an explanation of the incentives that produce it, and the interventions CHT advances with different actors.

This approach accomplishes two things:

- **It honors CHT’s systemic view of technology** — focusing on incentives, structures, and design principles rather than individual villains.
- **It makes the issues emotionally and intellectually graspable** for people who care deeply but may not yet have the language for what they’re experiencing.

To support this, we worked with CHT to create a simple circular visualization of their theory of change — showing how the organization creates clarity, shapes incentives, and supports shifts in norms. The framework gives visitors a way to understand how the pieces fit together without needing to master the entire field at once.

### Tone, Design, and the Feeling of the Place

CHT came into the project with a clearly articulated voice: confident and human, direct without contempt, urgent without alarmism, inspiring without polarization, and educational without losing people in abstraction. Every design and content decision flowed from that foundation.

The visual language — color, typography, spacing, illustration — supports a feeling of seriousness without sterility. Photography and warmer elements keep the human stakes visible. The copy uses an inclusive “we” that positions visitors not as observers but as participants in a shared effort.

The goal was to create a place people feel they can return to whenever society reaches another technological crossroads — a site that holds clarity, reason, and moral steadiness.

### Infrastructure for a Moving Target

CHT works in an environment where issues evolve quickly and unpredictably. The site therefore had to be designed not as a static artifact, but as flexible infrastructure.

We built:

- A modular campaign system with activatable banners for urgent public moments.
- Reusable campaign templates for future initiatives, including the impact campaign around the forthcoming AI documentary.
- Structures that allow new analysis, research, and content to surface immediately through the homepage and navigation.

A defining element of this infrastructure is the [**“AI in Society” narrative page**](https://www.humanetech.com/ai-society), which became one of the most distinctive pieces of the project. Through animation, storytelling, and a cohesive design system, the page expresses CHT’s understanding of AI’s societal impact in a way that is both emotionally resonant and coherent. It moves through the dynamics of incentives, power, and system design with clarity, helping visitors feel the stakes without oversimplifying them.

We also integrated CHT’s Substack directly into the site, unifying their main channel for thought leadership with their institutional presence. This allows visitors to encounter CHT’s ongoing commentary and deeper analysis in the same space, without requiring staff to maintain parallel content systems or duplicate work. It gives the site a living pulse and strengthens the continuity of the organization’s voice.

Beneath this sits a performance-oriented, mobile-first technical architecture designed for global reach, but built with usability for CHT’s staff at its core. Non-technical team members can update and expand content quickly, which is essential for an organization working in a fast-moving field.

### What Changed

Internally, the site has given CHT a clearer expression of who they have become. It reflects the organization as a deep, interdisciplinary institution. Different teams now see their work represented as part of a coherent whole. In post-launch feedback, CHT leadership described the site as something that helps unify internal understanding — clarifying the theory of change, grounding discussions with the board, and strengthening how the organization presents itself externally.

Externally, the site now serves the ecosystem more effectively:

- Policy advisors use the frameworks and issue pages to understand complex technological dynamics when drafting or advocating for legislation.
- Funders gain a clearer view of CHT’s systemic leverage — enabling more thoughtful and long-term investment decisions.
- Technologists can see how their decisions connect to broader harms and where reform might begin.
- Parents, educators, and concerned citizens find language and orientation for the unease they already felt.
- Journalists and amplifiers have a structured resource for understanding and conveying the stakes.

The site also generates a steady flow of people looking to do more — validating that CHT’s work continues to resonate widely.

### Building for the AI Age

This project illustrates a broader truth about democratic life in an era shaped by artificial intelligence and automated systems. Movements need institutions that can:

- help people understand what is happening to them,
- articulate the structures shaping their lives, and
- point toward meaningful, collective paths forward.

That work requires real infrastructure — not ephemeral campaigns. It requires places where analysis, narrative, and agency come together.

The Center for Humane Technology now has a platform that expresses its role as a bridge between tech, policy, and the public, and that can grow alongside the movement for humane technology. It is a foundation for helping people understand and shape a technological future rooted in human dignity, wisdom, and democratic possibility.
