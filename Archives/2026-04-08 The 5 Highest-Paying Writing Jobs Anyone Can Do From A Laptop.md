## 1\. Ghostwriting Educational Email Courses

First, what is an Educational Email Course (EEC)?

This is a high-value opt-in, sent to a lead over a number of days via email, that businesses, creators, and industry experts can use as a way to attract subscribers to their email list.

We use them across our business verticals:

- [Write With AI](https://startwritingwithai.com/)
- [Start Writing Online](https://startwritingonline.com/)
- [Premium Ghostwriting Blueprint](https://premiumghostwritingblueprint.com/)

![Zdjęcie](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HFYhxnla8AEsE_g?format=png&name=large)

So, why is an EEC such a great service to offer?

For two reasons:

## Reason #1: It’s an in-demand service.

Every single business wants more potential customers.

But businesses do a terrible job of capturing traffic to either their social media profiles or on their website. They either have no opt-in or they offer something vague (i.e. a poorly packaged offer).

For example, these are common lead magnets people offer:

1. **A newsletter opt-in.** We’ve all seen a website with ”Sign up for updates!” stuck above an opt-in form. They’re going to lose a lot of traffic because this offer isn’t appealing. The person has no idea what they’re getting in return for handing over a piece of personal information (their email address!).
2. **A PDF download (like a cheat sheet, industry whitepaper, etc.).** The person knows what they’re getting but most businesses do a terrible job of framing the value of the offer. Plus, this is a one-time event (how many PDFs have you downloaded and never read? Exactly!). Whereas, an EEC keeps your client top of mind over a number of days.
3. **A free trial.** This is actually a great lead magnet, but most companies that offer a free trial do a terrible job of making sure their new lead actually uses the product/service and gets value from it. Why? Because they never follow up! The solution is an EEC.

An EEC has a tangible offer and promises to deliver an outcome, which means the lead is more likely to type in their email address (and so your client gets more leads!).

Plus, you’re training people to open your client’s emails because they want to get the course (and it’s not just another spammy newsletter).

## Reason #2: It’s a skill builder.

Secondly, It helps you build a ton of skills you can apply across many different kinds of services:

- **Offer Creation.** You’re really getting paid to come up with an offer. You have to make it so good that people are willing to type in their email and receive the course.
- **Information Architecture.** You have to think about how to assemble information to deliver an outcome for the reader. How do you structure that over 5-7 days?
- **Copywriting.** Finally, you also get to practice subtle copywriting by nudging the lead to take the next step (whether that’s a service or a product) with your client after they’ve finished the email course.

Plus tons more (like formatting, subject line writing, automation building, etc.).

If you can build an EEC, congratulations! You now you have 10 different skills that you can monetize for the rest of your life.

# 2\. Ghostwriting a Weekly Newsletter

This is the natural next step after an EEC.

Most businesses don’t realize what their email list actually is: people raising their hand and saying, “Yes, I want to hear more about your product and/or service!”

But a lot of businesses collect leads but fail to email them to continue the nurturing process and gently nudge the lead into taking the next step.

It’s crucial you keep following up with more and more value.

The problem is that the vast majority of businesses and creators actually don't understand this concept.

They have one faulty belief:

## They think if they share too many things for free, they won’t buy their product or need their service.

Well, the opposite is true:

- The more stuff you give away for free…
- The more people associate that value with you…
- The more likely they are to become a customer.

Keep telling them about a new problem they didn't know about, answer common questions, or show them how to achieve another outcome, and they're more likely to become a customer, not less.

# 3\. Ghostwriting Social Media Content

So, your customer has an EEC and a weekly newsletter—but how are they attracting traffic to their opt-in?

This is where a ghostwriter gets hired to create content for platforms like X (formerly Twitter) and LinkedIn.

Right now, these two platforms have the best distribution and are the largest platforms for professionals and business owners.

Five years ago, it might have been Quora and Medium (which have now fizzled out but this is where I used to write for my clients). And five years from now it could be different platforms.

## But the rules of the game don't change.

Once you can write on one of these platforms, you can write on all of them—once you learn the certain styles and formats best suited to the platform.

Here’s what most people don’t understand about social content. You’re not just writing random posts.

Instead, you’re scaling someone else's insights.

As a Social Media Ghostwriter, you’re working with someone smart (usually a founder, business owner, or investor).

All you do is:

- Extract their existing insights and expertise
- Break those insights into bite-sized content pieces
- Help them build their personal brand consistently

Now, there are two ways to extract content from your client:

- **Method 1: Existing Materials.** This is books they've written, podcast episodes they've recorded, keynote speeches they've given, or internal company training materials (a hidden goldmine!)
- **Method 2: Interviews.** 70% of my clients had no existing content. So I would schedule 30-minute calls weekly or bi-weekly. I’d ask targeted questions to extract what I needed. I recorded everything (tools like Fathom make this easier than ever). The client shares their expertise, and I would do the rest.

In short, you’re not really writing.

You’re editing. You’re repackaging the existing content. You’re editing the interview transcript. You’re formatting it for the platform (X or LinkedIn).

# 4\. Ghostwriting Thought Leadership Articles

These are 800-1200 word articles that position your client as a leader in their space.

But who needs thought leadership articles?

Companies in forward-leaning spaces are desperate for this content:

- AI companies
- Crypto/Web3 businesses
- Biotech firms
- FinTech startups

In short, any business wanting to be seen as innovative

## They want to signal they're at the forefront of their industry, and articles help them do exactly that.

Plus, social media content can only go so far in achieving this. These articles are a deeper exploration, giving a comprehensive deep dive into a topic.

They have other benefits, too:

1. **SEO.** These articles can rank for keywords and drive organic traffic.
2. **Repurposing potential.** One article can be broken down into multiple social posts.
3. **Network expansion.** Articles can be published on industry publications, expanding your client's reach.

And the process for extracting content from your client is similar to Social Media ghostwriting.

My ghostwriting process was similar to the social media content:

- Extract insights through interviews or existing materials
- Structure them into a coherent narrative
- Add supporting evidence (research, case studies, examples)
- Edit for clarity and voice
- Place on the client's blog or submit to industry publications

This is a natural upsell after you've been ghostwriting social content for a client. After capturing their voice and understanding their expertise, thought leadership articles are a logical next step.

**What To Charge: $1,000-3,000 per article**

This content matters because, for many potential customers, the website is their first interaction with the business.

Good website copy explains what the business does in simple terms and it helps separate the business from competitors.

Ultimately, website copy should turn visitors into leads or customers.
