Last year, we [predicted](http://google.com/url?q=https://www.mrss.com/lab/four-scary-predictions-for-digital-advertising-in-2026/&sa=D&source=docs&ust=1773169835844656&usg=AOvVaw3aOwPzjgzYWnYnADTJ0Oqx) that by this time Google would have fully shifted to an AI-first experience and ChatGPT would roll out some sort of ads offering. Were we right? Yes on ChatGPT, not quite on Google. But let’s dive into the details:

As users shift to getting answers from Google’s AI Overviews or AI Mode — or increasingly using LLMs instead of search engines altogether — the way they interact with organic and paid search is evolving. Users are shifting from searching a handful of keywords that result in a long page of blue links to receiving a full answer to their query in an AI overview with no need to click to a website, or searching in paragraphs and having back-and-forth “discussions” with their search tools.

#### Google Ads: Where Are We Now?

Google’s AI Mode is not the default yet, and standard Google results still show up in [52% of all queries](https://www.searchenginejournal.com/google-ai-overviews-surges-across-9-industries/568448/#:~:text=New%20research%20from%20BrightEdge%20shows,Grows%2052%25%20In%20One%20Year). However, AI Overview coverage has increased [58% from Feb 2025 to Feb 2026,](https://www.searchenginejournal.com/google-ai-overviews-surges-across-9-industries/568448/#:~:text=New%20research%20from%20BrightEdge%20shows,Grows%2052%25%20In%20One%20Year) so we anticipate AI Overviews will cross the halfway point soon, with informational searches being affected the most. So, the AI search takeover is more of a slow burn (rather than the sudden shift we predicted).

As LLM search is gaining popularity, many users are using answer engines as a discovery tool without ever clicking to a website, looking at a source, or scrolling down to see the non-AI search results. They’re starting and ending their journey all within one conversation.

Here’s what all of this means for paid search.

#### Hard truth #1: Your search ads are being seen less and less, and to get them seen, Google wants you to use its AI tools more and more.

Traditional search ads are being pushed further down the page, by the AI Overview box. Here’s what that looks like in practice:

![](https://www.mrss.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/AEO-search-blog-images-03.png)

**How to adapt:** Make sure you’re using broad match, AI Max, or Performance Max. These bidding strategies are the only way to make your ads appear within AI Overviews. *Note that this is not currently available for political advertisers.*

- *Another note: If you are currently just running only brand-focused campaigns, opening up to these bidding strategies will expand the queries you appear on to non-brand terms as well. So there will likely be a tradeoff between appearing in AI Overviews vs potential performance implications for expanding to non-brand terms.*

#### Hard truth #2: Searches are becoming conversations, not a few keywords in a box.

As users adapt to the new search conversation style, they are likely to shift their behavior and search transactional keywords (like donate terms and brand terms) less and less over time. We anticipate a shift to more informational queries (ex. What can I do to support reproductive rights?) in LLM’s and AIO/AI Mode.

**How to adapt:** In your paid search campaigns, continue to maximize brand-focused keywords/campaigns as much as you can while the volume is still there, while also building your campaign setup to capture the shift in user queries, such as piloting AI Max and launching non-brand campaigns.

At the same time, make sure your brand is appearing how you want it to in AI Overviews when people search for your organization. This is a key part of what’s known as Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) — the new toolset for ensuring visibility in an AI-first search landscape. [We’ve talked a bit about this](https://www.mrss.com/lab/searches-up-traffic-down-how-the-ai-search-cliff-impacted-17-nonprofits/) ([and it’s impact at EOY](http://google.com/url?q=https://www.mrss.com/lab/the-impact-of-ai-search-during-end-of-year-the-news-isnt-good/&sa=D&source=docs&ust=1775062396928480&usg=AOvVaw1j9qxga13pmdgus0X5ghEY)), but it boils down to strategies including:

- Study organic and LLM Traffic trends to your website
- Monitor what appears in AI overviews and on LLM chatbots for key brand and non-brand searches, and what sources are being cited. (It’s possible that ancient annual report buried deep in your website or someone’s old Reddit post is informing what shows up on the AI overview for people who search your brand!)
- Optimize your website content for AI Search, and implement earned and social media strategies to gain more third party content for answer engines to pull form.
	- **Need help with this? M+R has an AEO team! Reach out to** [**aeo@mrss.com**](mailto:aeo@mrss.com)
- If your organization isn’t showing up in competitive non-brand prompts, identify which queries your brand is not appearing in and ensure that you have a paid search ads campaign serving to those queries/keywords.
	- To use the International Relief Organizations prompt from above as an example, if you are an international relief organization and the AI Overview did not list your company in their response, ensure that you are bidding on “International Relief” broad match/AI max keywords to help ensure your brand appears near your competitors.

#### Ads Within ChatGPT and Other LLMs: Where Are We Now?

This one we got right – ChatGPT rolled out an ads offering last month. However, they are starting with a high commitment of $200K and an outrageous $60 CPM – treating their initial rollout of ads more like a linear TV buy than a search product. We are curious to see where this goes, but expect it will be a while before their offering is optimized and designed for direct response (though based on their [open job descriptions](https://digiday.com/media-buying/openai-is-b-building-the-ad-tech-stack-its-currently-borrowing/?utm_campaign=digidaydis&utm_source=daily&utm_content=260310), it looks like they are quickly looking to enhance their ads).

In terms of the other major players:

- Claude has confirmed they are not planning to roll out an ad product (and ironically are running an [ad campaign](http://youtube.com/watch?v=3sVD3aG_azw&list=PLf2m23nhTg1OW258b3XBiJME7tgrRk-KI&index=4) trolling ChatGPT about it).
- Perplexity piloted an ad offering last year but has since abandoned it.

**Final Thoughts:** As we write this, we are operating in a hybrid world where traditional search and AI search coexist. What that means for nonprofit advertisers is clear: diversification is no longer optional. While we continue to maximize the high-intent traffic remaining in standard Google results, the future belongs to those who invest early in bridging the gap between paid visibility (AI Max/Performance Max) and organic authority (Answer Engine Optimization).

The later you start monitoring how your brand and issues appear to your donors and advocates reaching you through AI – and adjusting your strategy accordingly – the more likely it is you’ll be left behind.
