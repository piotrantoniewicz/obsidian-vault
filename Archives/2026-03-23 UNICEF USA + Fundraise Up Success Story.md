## About UNICEF USA

UNICEF USA braves the world’s toughest places to protect the rights of every child, everywhere. Across 190 countries and territories, the organization is on the ground, bringing life-saving help and hope to families affected by humanitarian emergencies. Founded more than 70 years ago, UNICEF USA has always made the rights, safety, and well-being of children its mission critical.

## The challenge: Launch a digital-first brand strategy

#### A pivot to digital offers promise and opportunity

In 2018, UNICEF USA pivoted to a digital-first approach. As many NPOs can attest to, the world of charitable giving was changing. And with the competitive landscape becoming more crowded, UNICEF USA had a multi-pronged challenge to grow its brand cost-effectively, secure more donor wallet share, manage rising costs, and reach new donors.

Helene Vallone, Vice President, Direct Response for UNICEF USA reflects on the decision to bank on digital. “At the time, we were heavily focused on monthly giving. And while our revenue was growing, our file of sustainers was shrinking.”

![](https://ucarecdn.com/e7aa43b6-ea11-49bf-b517-80f2b23b4bcc/-/format/auto/-/resize/96x96/)

Helene Vallone Vice President, Direct Response

To reach the organization’s ambitious goals, there was a long list of requirements to fulfill. But it was a necessary decision to improve changing donor expectations, maximize donations, and increase monthly donorship.

## The solution: A digital-first donation platform

The shift in strategy meant UNICEF USA needed to invest in a new, innovative technology stack — including a new donation platform.

At the time of UNICEF USA’s pivot, Fundraise Up was a young disruptor to the nonprofit software scene. It was eager to prove its chops with a powerhouse like UNICEF USA, the world’s farthest-reaching humanitarian organization for children, with annual contributions in the billions.

Reliability was a big factor as the organization evaluated new technology. For a global brand with the stature of UNICEF USA, a system malfunction would directly impact decades of earned trust for their mission.

Says Helene, “Over my career, I’ve had experiences with many donation platforms and have seen their failings. So the reliability and stability of a donation platform, and its ability to scale effortlessly to handle volume from thousands to hundreds of thousands, is a critical factor for investment.”

Fundraise Up committed to having UNICEF USA up and running on its platform in a mere six weeks, and there were concerns about meeting this timeline with a startup company.

“There was a lot of hesitation,” confesses Helene. “We have very stringent practices surrounding vendors. Internal conversations were very challenging, and there were many questions for the Fundraise Up team.”

But with [Fundraise Up’s AI-driven capabilities](https://fundraiseup.com/platform/fundraising-ai/), including features such as dynamic donation asks, seamless upselling to recurring donations, and a user-friendly interface, UNICEF’s interest was peaked. And with a successful A/B test of the platform's performance, a decision was made — UNICEF USA and Fundraise Up would partner.

## Up and successfully running in just six weeks

From the get-go, Fundraise Up delivered on every promise, with the team handling UNICEF USA’s rigorous due diligence and an aggressive implementation deadline with complete professionalism.

“That truly is the basis of our story,” Helene reflects. “Fundraise up delivered, and continues to deliver on its promises. It is an organization that’s truly client-focused.”
