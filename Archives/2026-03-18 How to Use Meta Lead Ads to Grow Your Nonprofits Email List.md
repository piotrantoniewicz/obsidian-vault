Meta Lead Ads are a great way for nonprofits of all sizes and budgets to grow their email lists, and with 33% of online donors saying that email is the communication method that most often inspires them to make a donation, prioritizing growing your nonprofit's email list in 2026 is the best way to raise more money online.

The most recent edition of the M+R Benchmarks Report revealed that the average cost per lead (CPL) from Meta Platforms, i.e., Facebook, Instagram, Threads, and WhatsApp, is $3.20 utilizing all Meta ad formats. However, Meta Lead Ads can cost significantly less, provided you create a compelling lead ad.

## Step 1: Set up the lead ad.

As an admin for your nonprofit's Facebook page, go to Meta Ads Manager > Create > Leads. Name your campaign, such as "Grow [Your Nonprofit] Email List" and set a daily budget (minimum $3/day). For "Conversion location," select "Instant forms." Select your "Audience" — the more broad, the more cost-effective. Under "Placements," select the Platforms where you want your lead ad displayed.

## Step 2: Set up the lead ad form.

Select "Create form" and configure contact fields (first name, last name, email). Leads can be stored in Facebook, Google Sheets, or sent directly to your CRM. Add a headline, description, and thank-you screen.

## Step 3: Create your ad.

Upload a graphic (1080x1080 or 1080x540 px), write primary text to entice subscriptions, add a headline, and select the "Sign up" call to action.

## Step 4: Publish and monitor your ad in Meta Ads Manager.

Initial CPL will range $2–5 depending on audience breadth. CPL decreases as Meta learns who to target. If CPL doesn't decrease over time, A/B test different graphics, text, and audiences.

## Step 5: Download your leads.

At least once a week, download new leads from Meta Business Suite > Leads Center and add them to your email list. Place new leads in an email automation welcoming them to your community.
