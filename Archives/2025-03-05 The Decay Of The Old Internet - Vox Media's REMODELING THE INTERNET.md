I recently came across an interesting [study](https://www.theverge.com/press-room/617654/internet-community-future-research) of 2000 US adults questioning the shift in the use and trust of the major internet tools and where trust lies.

My TLDR

- There are concerns over trust and the quality of Google
- Most understand Google is an ad-driven model and sponsored results aren’t always helpful
- There is a movement to people I know and trust (on platforms like Discord, Patreon and Substack)
- The impact from social media is felt and is seen as a negative
- Big platforms are slowly losing their trust
- People are seeking out community (and go niche where possible)
- Over half are asking communities/smaller groups and use tools like TikTok for answers (vs search engines)
- Many (over half) want more in-person connection

**Interesting & Key Takeaways**

- 60% would equate the state of social media negatively,
	- Stating: They feel like a number in a giant algorithmic machine
		- A place of never-ending product placements
		- A place where I’m a data point waiting to be sold

**Community**

- 75% of people would agree that the digital world has replaced face-to-face interaction
- Where do they gather information from
	- Discord 22%
		- Patreon - 9%
		- Substack - 6%
		- Fediverse - 4%
- 9 in 10 feel news and social media are creating an echo chamber effect, leading to deeper societal divisions
- 53% believe communities should be no more than 200 people digitally
- How respondents see ways to engage in communities
	- Sharing content within the community 54%
		- Commenting on posts/contributing to the chatter 53%
		- Liking or reacting to posts 50%
		- Creating original posts or content 45%
- 44% crave more human-driven, less algorithm-dependent content
- 38% want increased privacy and data protection
- 86% of people crave smaller, more personal digital spaces with those they care about

**The Google Search Impact**

- 42% said Google (and search engines) are becoming less useful.
- 76% said that more than a quarter of Google Search shopping results appear to “be sponsored or promoted.”
	- 14% described sponsored or promoted results as “ *very helpful*.”
- 55% said they get information from their community more than search.
- 52% said they use AI chatbots or alternative platforms (like TikTok) for information rather than Google Search.
- 66% said the quality of information is deteriorating, and question the reliability of its sources.
