You’ve done the hard work on email deliverability: [DMARC](https://dmarc.org/overview/?utm_campaign=bimi-why-your-logo-belongs-in-the-inbox&utm_medium=referral&utm_source=www.civicshoutnewsletter.com) is enforced, [SPF and DKIM are clean](https://www.truesense.com/blog/boost-your-nonprofits-email-reach-a-beginners-guide-to-spf-dkim-dmarc-and-bimi?utm_campaign=bimi-why-your-logo-belongs-in-the-inbox&utm_medium=referral&utm_source=www.civicshoutnewsletter.com), and your messages are reaching supporters.

But your emails still look like everybody else’s. Just a gray initial next to your name rather than your cause’s shiny icon.

That’s the gap BIMI fills. And a change in the last few years makes it much more accessible for nonprofits to implement.

**What BIMI does**  
BIMI (Brand Indicators for Message Identification) puts your logo next to your emails in Gmail, Yahoo, Apple Mail, and others.

If your domain passes DMARC, you publish a record that tells inbox providers where to find your logo.

The data for *why* this matters is mostly from commercial senders, but nonprofits should pay attention.

Yahoo’s pilot found [10% higher engagement](https://emailexpert.com/consumer-reactions-to-email-logos-bimi/?utm_campaign=bimi-why-your-logo-belongs-in-the-inbox&utm_medium=referral&utm_source=www.civicshoutnewsletter.com) for BIMI-enabled emails.

A Red Sift and Entrust study of over 1,000 adults found open rates (even as unreliable as they can be) [climbed as high as 39%](https://redsift.com/guides/consumer-interaction-with-visual-brands-in-email?utm_campaign=bimi-why-your-logo-belongs-in-the-inbox&utm_medium=referral&utm_source=www.civicshoutnewsletter.com) when a logo was present, regardless of brand size.

Think about it: When someone donates to your organization, they’re extending trust. A familiar logo reinforces that every time you show up in their inbox.

**Common Mark Certificates**  
Until recently, getting your logo to display in Gmail required a [Verified Mark Certificate](https://www.digicert.com/faq/email-trust/what-is-a-verified-mark-certificate?utm_campaign=bimi-why-your-logo-belongs-in-the-inbox&utm_medium=referral&utm_source=www.civicshoutnewsletter.com), which meant a registered trademark.

For many nonprofits, that’s a non-starter.

In September 2024, Google started accepting [Common Mark Certificates.](https://bimigroup.org/announcing-common-mark-certificates/?utm_campaign=bimi-why-your-logo-belongs-in-the-inbox&utm_medium=referral&utm_source=www.civicshoutnewsletter.com) A CMC doesn’t require a trademark — just proof your logo has been in use for at least a year.

You won’t get Gmail’s blue verified checkmark (which still requires a VMC), but your logo will still display.

Apple Mail still requires a VMC. But a CMC covers Gmail, and Yahoo already covers logos for senders with a good reputation, so you’re reaching a good bulk of inboxes.

A CMC costs around $1,100 per year. That can be a lot for a nonprofit, but consider what you’re getting — every email you send becomes a branded impression at basically zero.

Your logo next to a fundraising appeal is reaching people who already choose to hear from you. Even a modest improvement in engagement adds up fast.

**How to get started**  
The SSL Store has a comprehensive, easy-to-follow guide to [BIMI setup and implementation.](https://www.thesslstore.com/blog/how-to-set-up-bimi-and-a-mark-certificate-to-display-your-email-logo/?utm_campaign=bimi-why-your-logo-belongs-in-the-inbox&utm_medium=referral&utm_source=www.civicshoutnewsletter.com)

First, make sure your DMARC policy is set to quarantine or reject — if you’re still at “none,” [start there.](https://dmarc.org/overview/?utm_campaign=bimi-why-your-logo-belongs-in-the-inbox&utm_medium=referral&utm_source=www.civicshoutnewsletter.com)

Then, get your logo into SVG Tiny P/S format, apply for a CMC through [DigiCert](http://digicert.com/tls-ssl/verified-mark-certificates?utm_campaign=bimi-why-your-logo-belongs-in-the-inbox&utm_medium=referral&utm_source=www.civicshoutnewsletter.com), and publish a BIMI DNS record.

Not sure where you stand on BIMI at all? Red Sift has a [free BIMI checker.](https://redsift.com/tools/bimi?utm_campaign=bimi-why-your-logo-belongs-in-the-inbox&utm_medium=referral&utm_source=www.civicshoutnewsletter.com)

**The bottom line**  
Let me be clear, BIMI won’t save a struggling email program.

But if your authentication is solid and you’re fighting for attention in packed inboxes, getting your logo into every send is definitely one of the easier wins.
