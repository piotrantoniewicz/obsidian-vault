What do your birthday, DNA and phone password all have in common? No, this isn’t a trick question — the answer is they don’t change (or rarely do). The same is true for evergreen marketing.

Unlike typical fast-moving, time-sensitive marketing content, evergreen content stays relevant and useful, no matter how much time passes. This type of content has little to no risk of fading away or becoming outdated, making it a powerful tool for long-term organic growth and thought leadership for your brand.

Want to learn the ins and outs of evergreen marketing? This guide will cover using it in both marketing and sales, its benefits, examples of it in action and best practices for creating your own.

## The Fast-Moving Nature of Marketing

It’s no surprise that marketing moves quickly. At its core, it’s about anticipating and satisfying customer needs, meaning it requires having your finger on the pulse of what’s going on. And with trends, current events and algorithms rapidly changing, marketers must often act as sprinters rather than long-distance runners to get word to customers faster than the competition. For instance, newsjacking, seasonal campaigns and trend-based posts.

However, the reality of the quick environment is that you put in tons of effort for content that will have a short lifespan and eventually lose traffic after relevance fades. This is simply an unavoidable thing as a marketer. That’s why it’s also essential to tie traditional timely work with evergreen pieces.

Success often lies in learning to walk the tightrope between timely marketing work and evergreen content, balancing between what’s important right now and what will remain critical for years.

## What’s Evergreen Marketing?

[Evergreen marketing](https://www.brafton.com/blog/content-writing/what-is-an-evergreen-story/) is a type of advertising that uses content, campaigns and strategies that remain continuously relevant and valuable, despite how long it lives. Its goal is to compound traffic, creating a steady, sustainable stream of search traffic.

Like the trees that this marketing strategy gets its name from, evergreen content stays fresh and useful long past its publication. It’s marked by its:

- Timelessness.
- Long-term relevance.
- Lack of an expiration date.

### Evergreen vs. Time-Sensitive Content

If we compared content to plants, time-sensitive content would be gorgeous tulips that are stunning for a season and quickly wither. On the other hand, evergreen content is like a live oak tree that never goes dormant or changes colors in the fall. Both are beautiful, but are expected to serve different purposes.

The same can be said for evergreen and time-sensitive copy. Fast-paced, trend-focused content is designed to capture attention in the moment, ride the momentum of what’s currently popular and drive immediate engagement or conversions. Evergreen content’s role is to be a consistent source of helpful information that’s always available when and where people need it, slowly but surely drawing in traffic and nurturing leads toward a final CTA.

Here are some [examples of evergreen content](https://www.brafton.com/blog/content-writing/evergreen-content-examples/) formats you should be aware of:

- How-to guides and tutorials.
- FAQs and answers to common questions.
- Checklists and templates.
- Case studies.
- Product reviews.
- In-depth articles on specific topics.
- Infographics and step-by-step resources.

If you’re looking to create long-lasting content, start with beginner’s guide posts, how-to tutorials and industry definitions and glossaries, modifying these examples so they feel authentic and on brand.

## What Does Evergreen Mean in Sales?

The best thing evergreen content can do for your business is continue selling without you manually doing all of the work. Instead of just relying on seasonal campaigns or one-time pushes, evergreen sales strategies and assets stay on-brand and deliver value long after they’re created. It focuses on materials and systems that constantly generate leads, support conversions and move potential customers through the funnel over time.

Picture it like this: A flash sale vs. a well-built storefront. One drives a burst of activity, while the other keeps attracting and converting customers day after day. These evergreen sales strategies usually mix foundational content and automated systems that support your sales team at every stage of the buyer journey. Popular examples include:

- Sales decks and brochures that clearly explain your value proposition and can be reused across different conversations.
- Email marketing sequences that nurture leads automatically based on behavior and interest.
- Product demos and explainer content that help prospects understand your solution without needing live walkthroughs every time.
- Case studies and testimonials that continuously build trust by showcasing real-world results.

### What’s an Evergreen Promotion?

An evergreen promotion is a deal, offer or campaign that stays active over time rather than expiring after a certain window. Instead of being tied to a specific season, it’s always available or continuously running in the background. For example, always-on discounts or offers, automated email funnels or year-round lead generation campaigns.

## Why Create Evergreen Content? Key Advantages

It’s a great question! If traditional marketing creates massive traffic spikes, why not just continuously create trend-focused campaigns? This is definitely one way to do your marketing, but it leaves room for gaps to grow.

Evergreen marketing brings a lot to the table, and avoiding it can lead to missing out on some pretty helpful benefits:

### Long-Term Value

Think of your absolute best friend in the world — that relationship didn’t form overnight, right? It took months (or years) of quality time and getting to know one another. Evergreen content helps you create lasting relationships with your customers, letting your brand step into a trusted role of thought leader and helper, delivering long-term value and growing your “friend group” in an authentic way.

### Consistent Organic Traffic

When you check your website traffic, it often looks like a roller coaster with lots of ups, downs and loops. Rather than relying solely on timely campaigns to create surges of website visitors over a short time, pairing those efforts with sustainable content keeps people coming regularly without any additional effort or money on your part.

### Continuous Lead Generation

By putting out a steady stream of well-crafted evergreen posts and driving traffic to your site regularly, you build site authority and brand awareness, ultimately leading to more qualified leads. This lets your content work for you while you focus on more time-sensitive topics.

### Brand Authority and Trust

Would you rather trust someone you just met or an expert on the topic you’re wondering about? It’s safe to assume you’d choose to listen to the person with authority, right? When you focus on building a bank of deeply insightful, data-driven evergreen content, people, search engines and now especially AI answer engines, will notice and put confidence in you as an expert in your field.

### Content Repurposing Support

One of the biggest hidden advantages of evergreen content is how easy it is to [reuse and reshape](https://www.brafton.com/blog/strategy/repurposing-content-strategy/) across your marketing channels. A single high-quality blog post can become multiple social media posts, an email newsletter, an infographic, a webinar topic or a downloadable guide. Because evergreen content stays relevant over time, you can refresh and redistribute it without constantly starting from scratch.

## Putting It Into Practice: How To Create Long-Lasting Content

It’s one thing to talk about evergreen content, but let’s look at how to put pen to paper (or hands to keyboard). Here’s a step-by-step guide to get you started:

### Step 1: Choose the Right Topics

Not all subjects are meant to be evergreen, and that’s a good thing. However, ensure you come up with evergreen topics that won’t lose their relevance after a few months. Ask yourself:

- What questions do customers always ask? Would a how-to guide be useful for them?
- What are the core solutions and differentiators you offer? Could a video series on these items be valuable?
- Do people often wonder what goes on behind the scenes at your brand? Would a peek behind the curtains in a blog format be intriguing to readers?
- What topics does your brand know a lot about? Could you become thought leaders in these areas?

### Step 2: Conduct Thorough Keyword Research

Once you’ve nailed down the evergreen topics, it’s time to narrow them down to value-driven keywords. Focus on search terms that have consistent interest over time rather than temporary spikes in popularity. Tools like Google Keyword Planner, SEMrush or Ahrefs can be useful.

Look for keywords that balance strong search volume with realistic competition levels, and don’t forget about long-tail keywords. These more specific phrases often attract highly qualified readers who are looking for direct answers or solutions. Most importantly, make sure the keywords align naturally with user intent; your content should solve a problem, answer a question or provide meaningful insight.

### Step 3: Create High-Quality, In-Depth Content

Content can only be evergreen if it can withstand the test of time. That means quality, quality, quality. Rather than creating short, surface-level articles, aim to produce comprehensive resources that fully cover the topic. Think guides, tutorials, FAQs, case studies or expert insights that readers can return to repeatedly.

### Step 4: Optimize for SEO

No [editorial content](https://www.brafton.com/blog/content-writing/editorial-content/) should be published without first fine-tuning for search engines and AI visibility. Optimize your title tags, meta descriptions, headers, and image alt text with your target keywords while still keeping the writing natural and reader-friendly. Also, update internal links with recent additions to your library, continuously feeding people from one asset to the next.

### Step 5: Keep Content Updated

Even evergreen content needs a bit of polishing every so often. Algorithms change, and it’s essential to [re-optimize and keep things fresh](https://www.brafton.com/blog/strategy/historical-optimization/). Review your content periodically to ensure statistics, screenshots, links and best practices are still accurate and relevant.

### Step 6: Repurpose and Distribute

Don’t forget to get creative with your content and try out different use cases, from infographics to short-form videos and more. Don’t put yourself in a box — have fun with all the content types under the sun.
