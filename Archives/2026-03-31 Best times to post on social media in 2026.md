## How Sprout gathered the data

You need to know where this data comes from. To uncover how users engage with social content in 2026, we worked closely with Sprout’s internal Data Science team to conduct a comprehensive analysis.

We reviewed nearly 2 billion engagements across roughly 307,000 social profiles from Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, Pinterest, TikTok and X (formerly Twitter). This data encompasses over 30,000 Sprout Social customers globally to ensure we accurately capture when content is most—and least—frequently engaged across every major platform and industry between November 27, 2025 and February 27, 2026.

**A note on methodology and time zones:** Data from Sprout Social includes users from various plan types, industries and locations. To ensure accuracy across a global user base, all times are recorded in Local Time rather than being tied to one specific hub like US Central Time. This means that a peak engagement window of 9 a.m. translates to 9 a.m. in Los Angeles, 9 a.m. in London and 9 a.m. in Sydney.

This aggregate data serves as a powerful baseline for your scheduling strategy, but you should test these times against your own account’s performance to find your optimal send times.

What counts as an engagement?

- **Reactions**: Likes and favorites
- **Comments**: Direct replies to public posts
- **Shares**: Retweets, Repins and native platform shares
- **Direct** Messages: Private inbound messages
- **Clicks**: Specific link clicks

## Best times to post on social media

**The overall best times to post on social media are Tuesdays and Wednesdays between 11 a.m. and 6 p.m. Local Time.**

When you look at the aggregate data across all platforms, a clear pattern emerges: **midday to late afternoon** is the prime time for capturing audience attention on social media.

While each network has its own unique nuances, posting during the workweek consistently yields the highest engagement. This aligns with typical digital behavior: People are scrolling during their lunch breaks, taking midafternoon mental breathers and checking their feeds as they wrap up the workday.

Here’s a high-level breakdown of overall social media engagement in 2026 to guide your scheduling strategy:

| Metric | 2026 Data Trends |
| --- | --- |
| **Best times to post on social media** | Tuesdays and Wednesdays, 12–6 p.m. Local Time. |
| **Best days to post on social media** | Tuesdays and Wednesdays. The mid-week momentum is real, with users highly active and readily interacting with brand content. |
| **Worst days to post on social media** | Sundays. Unplugging on the weekend translates directly to lower engagement scores across almost every single platform. |

If you are looking for a baseline scheduling strategy to build your [social media calendar](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/social-media-calendar/), aim for the middle of the day and middle of the week. But to truly maximize your reach, you need to [tailor your publishing cadence](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/how-often-to-post-on-social-media/) to the specific networks where your audience spends their time.

### Best times to post on visual-first networks (Facebook vs Instagram vs TikTok)

While aggregate data points to a midday, midweek peak, the way audiences consume content on **visual-first platforms** requires a more nuanced approach. [Short-form video (under 60 seconds) is the top-performing content format](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/data/2026-social-media-content-strategy-report/). Consuming it demands active attention—a stark contrast to the quick scanning behavior seen on text-heavy feeds.

| Network | Best Days | Best Times (Local Time) | Worst Days |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Facebook** | Mondays, Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Thursdays | - **Mondays:** 12–1 p.m. - **Tuesdays and Wednesdays:** 12–8 p.m. - **Thursdays:** 12–2 p.m. and 8 p.m. | Weekends, Early Mornings |
| **Instagram** | Mondays, Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Thursdays | - **Mondays:** 2–4 p.m. - **Tuesdays:** 1–7 p.m. - **Wednesdays:** 12–9 p.m. and 11 p.m. - **Thursdays:** 12–2 p.m. | Weekends, Early Mornings |
| **TikTok** | Mondays, Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Thursdays, Fridays | - **Mondays:** 3–5 p.m. - **Tuesdays:** 2–6 p.m. - **Wednesdays:** 1–8 p.m. - **Thursdays:** 1–5 p.m. - **Fridays:** 3–5 p.m. | Weekends, Early Mornings |

When comparing Facebook, Instagram and TikTok, we see distinct behavioral trends:

- **Facebook’s sustained afternoon wave:** Facebook engagement is concentrated in the afternoons but sustains remarkable momentum into the evening.
- **Instagram’s midday aesthetic break:** The app favors the midday slump, making the midmorning to early afternoon window (Mondays through Thursdays, starting around 12 p.m.) the undeniable sweet spot.
- **TikTok’s late-night immersion:** Engagement extends into late afternoons and early evenings (Tuesdays through Fridays, 2–6 p.m.), making those times ideal for posting immersive video content.

Understanding these subtle shifts allows you to sequence your visual content when your audience is online: hitting Instagram for the midday breather, capitalizing on Facebook’s afternoon-to-evening wave and dominating TikTok when users are ready to settle in and watch.

### Best times to post on text-first and B2B/B2C networks (X vs. LinkedIn)

The posting strategy shifts for text-centric networks. Both X (Twitter) and LinkedIn cater to B2B and B2C professional audiences, and the way those audiences consume information shows overlapping but concentrated peak engagement times.

| Network | Best Days | Best Times (Local Time) | Worst Days |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **X (Twitter)** | Mondays, Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Thursdays | - **Mondays:** 2–3 p.m. and 5 p.m. - **Tuesdays, Wednesdays and Thursdays:** 12–6 p.m. | Weekends, Early Mornings |
| **LinkedIn** | Mondays, Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Thursdays, Fridays | - **Mondays:** 1–2 p.m. - **Tuesdays:** 11 a.m.–5 p.m. - **Wednesdays:** 11 a.m. – 4 p.m. - **Thursdays:** 11 a.m. and 1–5 p.m. - **Fridays:** 11 a.m. and 1–2 p.m. | Weekends, Early Mornings |

Here is how the behavioral patterns break down between the two:

- **X (Twitter)’s real-time pulse:** Because it remains the source for real-time conversation and news, X sees high audience activity from midday through late afternoon (12–6 p.m.) Tuesday through Thursday, as people use it for a pulse-check.
- **LinkedIn’s morning momentum:** LinkedIn dominates the morning coffee and midday workday window, so Tuesdays and Thursdays late mornings are your best days for reaching decision-makers and professionals before they get pulled into deep-focus work.

Understanding these behaviors allows you to time your B2B and B2C content: capturing the morning B2B crowd with LinkedIn thought leadership, and joining X’s fast-paced afternoon wave with rapid-fire updates and cultural commentary.
