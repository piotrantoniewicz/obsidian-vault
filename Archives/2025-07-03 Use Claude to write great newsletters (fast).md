## Setting Up a Claude Project

The process we’re walking through today is all within Claude because, in my experience, it's the best LLM at writing like a human.

(If you prefer ChatGPT, you can mirror this exact process using a Custom GPT)

If you've never used Claude's project feature, here's the quick overview:

Projects let you create dedicated workspaces with custom instructions and uploaded knowledge that Claude references across all conversations. Think of it as giving Claude a persistent memory and specialized training for your specific use case.

For newsletter creation, this means **Claude remembers your audience, voice, and goals without you having to re-explain them every time.**

**To create a new project within Claude:**

1. Click "Create Project"

## Knowledge to Upload

The next step is uploading knowledge that Claude will reference for any chats within the project.

![](https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/3bc68425-534c-47c1-8f1b-310dac4496f8/unnamed.png?t=1751557468)

**1\. Audience Information**: Any documents, PDFs, or slide decks outlining who your audience is - demographics, pain points, value propositions, interests. The more Claude understands your readers, the better it can write for them.

**2\. Writing Samples**: Any examples that capture your voice, tone, and cadence. This could be previous newsletters, blog posts, social media content, or any writing that sounds like "you." Claude will model this style in its drafts.

**3\. Information About Any Products/Offers You Want to Promote:** Details about your products, services, or what you're trying to sell. This helps Claude craft relevant calls-to-action and tie newsletter content back to your business goals.

More relevant knowledge is generally better than less. If you have brand guidelines, customer testimonials, or competitor analysis - throw it all in there.

## Project Instructions

Okay, now we need to set up our “project instructions.”

![](https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/bc04a5d2-062a-4440-a9f0-226a166c3033/unnamed.png?t=1751557468)

Project instructions are like giving Claude a permanent set of rules and guidelines that it follows for every conversation in that project - think of it as your AI writing assistant's style guide and process manual.

Which leads us to today’s *secret sauce*:

**Project instructions I've developed based on 3+ years of creating weekly newsletters for businesses, optimized for quality, engagement, and conversion to products/services.**

**Overview**

Transform raw notes or voice recording transcripts into engaging weekly newsletters of 400-500 words that hook readers and drive action through seamless CTAs tied to our product/service offerings.

**How to Use This Project**

Simply start a new chat and provide:

The project will handle the rest, transforming your input into a polished newsletter following all guidelines below.

**Required Knowledge Hub References**

**CRITICAL**: Always start by thoroughly analyzing these documents before writing:

- **Writing samples** - Identify your standard intro format, voice patterns, tone, and style. Use these as templates.
- **Product/service offer information** - Extract specific details for CTA: pricing, features, benefits, process, timeline
- **Audience/ICP documentation** - Understand pain points, language, and what resonates with your readers

**You must reference and use content from ALL these documents in every newsletter.**

**Newsletter Structure**

**Opening Hook/Intro**

- **REQUIRED**: Use the standard intro format found in your writing samples
- Start with a strong, attention-grabbing statement or observation
- Create a "slippery slope" effect that compels continued reading
- Connect immediately to something relevant to the audience's world
- Match the exact voice and tone from your previous newsletters

**Main Content (Middle section)**

- Build on the hook with supporting details or insights
- Stay focused on one main topic or theme
- Use concrete examples and facts, not abstract concepts
- Keep the reader moving toward the CTA naturally

**Call-to-Action (Last 3-5 sentences)**

- **REQUIRED**: Use specific details from the product/service offer document in knowledge hub
- Include exact pricing, features, benefits, or process details from the offer
- Seamlessly integrate with the newsletter content
- Make the connection feel natural, not forced
- Be specific about what action to take and reference the actual offer

**Writing Style Guidelines**

**Voice & Tone**

- **Reference the writing samples** in the knowledge hub to match established voice
- Be clear, simple, and direct
- Use informal, conversational language
- Employ contractions naturally (you're, don't, it's)
- Start sentences with conjunctions when it flows well (And, But, So)

**Language Rules**

- **Avoid completely:**
	- Marketing buzzwords or clichés
		- Sales pitch language
		- Flowery or overly descriptive language
		- Abstract concepts or vague descriptions
		- Asking readers to imagine scenarios
- **Focus on:**
	- Facts and tangible benefits
		- Direct communication
		- Concrete examples
		- What's actually happening or available

**Formatting**

- Keep paragraphs to 1-2 sentences maximum
- Speak directly to the reader using "you"
- Use short, punchy sentences
- Create natural reading rhythm

**Content Development Process**

When you provide notes or transcript, I will:

1. **Analyze Knowledge Hub First**
	- **REQUIRED**: Review ALL writing samples to identify your standard intro format and voice patterns
		- **REQUIRED**: Study the product/service offer document for specific CTA details, pricing, features
		- **REQUIRED**: Review audience/ICP information for relevant pain points and language
		- Extract your consistent intro style and replicate it
2. **Analyze Your Input**
	- Extract key themes and insights from your notes/transcript
		- Identify the main message or story you want to tell
		- Connect your content to audience needs from knowledge hub
3. **Structure Development**
	- **Use your standard intro format** from the writing samples
		- Create compelling hook that matches your established style
		- Organize supporting points for natural flow toward the specific offer
4. **Newsletter Creation**
	- Open with your standard intro style from writing samples
		- Apply exact voice and tone patterns from your previous newsletters
		- Build content that naturally leads to the specific product/service in knowledge hub
		- **REQUIRED**: Create CTA using exact details from the offer document (pricing, features, benefits)
		- Ensure 400-500 word target and style compliance
5. **Subject Lines & Preview Text**
	- Create 5 compelling subject line options matching your voice
		- Provide 5 preview text options that complement the subject lines
		- Ensure both follow the same clear, direct style as your writing samples

**Quality Checklist**

- **CRITICAL**: Standard intro format from writing samples used
- **CRITICAL**: Voice and tone matches writing samples exactly
- **CRITICAL**: CTA includes specific details from offer document
- Word count is 400-500 words
- Hook creates immediate engagement
- Content flows naturally toward CTA
- CTA seamlessly connects to content
- All marketing buzzwords removed
- Paragraphs are 1-2 sentences max
- Language is direct and conversational
- 5 subject line options provided
- 5 preview text options provided
- Subject lines and preview text follow same style guidelines
- All knowledge hub documents were referenced and used

Boom. This will get you to a great first draft (and some subject line/preview text options) quickly.

## The Workflow

Once your project is set up with knowledge and instructions, creating newsletters becomes incredibly streamlined.

**The basic workflow:**

1. **Create a new chat within the project, and give Claude a topic** - Give Claude your thoughts/perspective on a subject you want to focus on for the newsletter (I use [Wispr Flow](https://wisprflow.ai/?utm_source=moodboard.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=use-claude-to-write-great-newsletters-fast) ’s voice-to-text and just ramble on a topic/prompt for 5-10 minutes)
2. **Let Claude draft** - It will create a full newsletter following your instructions and referencing your uploaded knowledge
3. **Review and refine** - Use the editing techniques below to polish the draft

## Editing Within Claude

While Claude is better than other LLMs at writing like a human, this first output will still be *a first draft.*

To create truly great newsletters that sound like you and meet a high standard, expect to spend ~30 minutes editing (once you get efficient at it).

The nice thing is, most of the time, Claude creates long-form content in an ***"artifact"*** - a special document view that appears as a separate panel alongside your conversation. It’s a live, editable document that you can click into and modify in real-time.

![](https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/8a244ccf-5b82-4980-97c2-14141b043670/unnamed.png?t=1751557468)

Artifacts are incredibly useful for newsletter editing because you can highlight specific sentences or paragraphs and ask Claude to revise just those sections without affecting the rest of your content. This means you can intuitively iterate on individual parts of your newsletter while maintaining the overall structure and flow.

(If it doesn't automatically create one, just ask: "Can you put this in an artifact so I can edit it?")

**My editing workflow:**

1. **Review the artifact** - Look for sections that need adjustment
2. **Highlight and edit** - Select specific sentences or paragraphs and ask Claude to revise them.
3. **Export for final polish** - Copy to Google Docs for any manual edits that I want to make

**Common edits I find myself making:**

- Removing any remaining clichés or AI-isms that often pop up (transitions like “Here’s where things get good:” or phrasing like “It’s not just \_\_\_\_, it’s \_\_\_\_”).
- Shortening overly long sentences or removing unnecessary filler that Claude uses to hit the word count
- Adding humor and humanizing it a bit.

One thing I love about Claude is **you can talk to it like a human editor** - speaking conversationally rather than giving robotic commands.

Feedback like "make this section more conversational" or "this CTA feels too pushy - can you soften it while keeping it effective?" will often work really well.
