Selling items for charity is one of the oldest and most effective fundraising approaches. Supporters buy something they want and, while doing so, support their church, PTA, kids’ team, or community nonprofit.

While people’s motivation to participate hasn’t changed, the process has. Most transactions are now electronic—via card, phone, or app—so relying on order forms and cash is limiting.

Now, let’s look at how an [online sales fundraiser](https://www.eventgroove.com/fundraising/sales/) transforms the process, making it more accessible and manageable for both organizers and supporters.

## How to Run an Online Sales Fundraiser

When supporters can buy from a link instead of a clipboard, the math changes:

- Reach goes well beyond your local community. A shareable page travels through email, group chats, and social posts. Grandparents in another state can buy as easily as the family next door.
- No cash to count, chase, or reconcile. Payments are processed securely through the platform, so you don’t have to match paper orders to cash envelopes at the end of the week.
- Real-time inventory and reporting. You can see what’s selling, who’s bought, and how close you are to your goal—without a spreadsheet.
- A smoother experience. A clean checkout and a clear cause beat tracking down the volunteer who has the order form every time.

## What You Can Sell

Pick something that speaks to your cause, has a broad enough appeal that supporters can give it as a gift, and lands at a price point your community is comfortable spending. Specific, story-driven [nonprofit merch](https://products.eventgroove.com/blog/articles/nonprofit-merch-t-shirt-fundraising-product-ideas/) tends to outsell generic gear by a wide margin.

Strong categories include:

- **Food and treats:** fudge and chocolates, baked goods, jams and sauces, BBQ, sub and pasta plates, and pancake breakfasts.
- **Seasonal favorites:** mums, spring flowers, holiday wreaths, wrapping paper, candles, and ornaments.
- **Branded apparel and merch:** [Custom hoodies](https://products.eventgroove.com/merchandise/tags/custom-hoodies-sweatshirts/) and tote bags are classic sellers for teams, PTAs, and clubs.
- **Drinkware:** Water bottles and tumblers, especially for 5K-style [A-thon fundraisers](https://www.eventgroove.com/fundraising/run-athons/).
- **Fun items:** Dog bandannas, t-shirts, socks, winter beanies, scarves, trucker caps—anything that pairs your cause with a memento.
- **Digital products:** printable cookbooks, recipe collections, or virtual classes.

## How to Run an Online Sales Fundraiser

### 1\. Define your goal and your audience

Are you raising funds for a specific trip, a piece of equipment, a scholarship, or an end-of-year campaign? Naming what your goal equates to sharpens every other decision: what to sell, what to charge, and how to talk about it. Audience matters too. A [school fundraiser](https://www.eventgroove.com/school-fundraiser-platform/) leans on parents, grandparents, and teachers while a sports-team fundraiser leans on players’ families plus the wider booster community. Meanwhile, an [makers market fundraiser](https://www.eventgroove.com/blog/creative-sale-fundraiser-ideas-host-a-makers-market/) counts on shoppers drawn to locally-made goods.

### 2\. Choose a fundraising platform built for sales

Choose a mobile-friendly sales fundraising platform with secure payments and an easy page builder. Ensure it offers simple checkout, clear reporting, donation options, and [transparent platform fees](https://www.eventgroove.com/fundraising/platform-pricing/) that won’t reduce your proceeds.

### 3\. Set up your sales fundraiser page

Your page is where you make a virtual case for your cause. So you’ll want it to make an emotional connection and be impactful. Here’s how:

- A clear, honest description of your cause and what the money will fund.
- Photos of who and what your fundraiser benefits.
- Sharp photos of every item, with options for sizes, colors, and quantities where they apply.
- A custom URL that’s easy to say out loud and type from a phone.
- Your branding—logo, colors, a hero image that looks like your group.

### 4\. Generate a QR code and printed promotion

Once the page is live, your platform should automatically generate a QR code that points straight to it. Drop the code onto printed promotional materials that reflect your brand and cause. For example, you’d order custom-printed [football flyers](https://products.eventgroove.com/flyers/tags/football/) for a team uniform drive, along with [awareness table tents](https://products.eventgroove.com/table-tents/tags/fundraiser/), yard signs, direct-mail [postcards](https://products.eventgroove.com/postcard-mailers/), and branded stickers you can apply to all sorts of surfaces. By combining cohesive visuals with QR codes, you’re making it easy for people to participate the moment your event is brought to their attention. The same playbook works beautifully for pancake breakfasts, bake sales, and spaghetti night fundraisers, which are among the most popular [QR code-powered food fundraisers](https://www.eventgroove.com/blog/how-to-use-qr-codes-to-cook-up-a-great-fundraiser-2/).

### 5\. Promote it everywhere your supporters already are

Share the link in email newsletters, social posts, group chats, and at every in-person moment your group already runs—games, services, meetings, and events. Links posted by volunteers within their own networks reliably outperform a single shared post from an organization’s account, so make it easy for them by providing ready-to-share images, caption suggestions, and the QR code in a couple of sizes.

### 6\. Fulfill cleanly and thank everyone

Decide up front whether items ship to the buyer or get picked up locally, and put that information on the page so there are no surprises. When the sale closes, communicate the total raised, deliver promptly, and thank every supporter—personally where you can. A good thank-you is the single best protection against [donor fatigue](https://www.eventgroove.com/fundraising-blog/5-strategies-to-combat-donor-fatigue-attract-new-donors/) and the foundation of every fundraiser that follows.
