The biannual Political Tech Landscape Report by [Higher Ground Labs](https://www.linkedin.com/company/highergroundlabs/) is a fascinating read that highlights the strengths and weaknesses of progressive approaches to digital tools (full report [here](https://docsend.com/view/f9huvk5k8ptct4b9)). After the painful loss at the voting booth in November, this year's analysis is particularly pertinent (yes I do like alliterations). Here are four key takeaways for political professionals:

**🧭 1. Massive Reach ≠ Real Engagement**

Despite record voter outreach—70% of voters contacted in battleground states—engagement *declined*, especially among young people.

**What this means for you:** You must move beyond volume. Invest in *relevance* —meet voters where they are, especially in digital and influencer spaces. Prioritise *resonance* over repetition.

---

**📉 2. The Right Wins on Organic Media—Despite Spending Less**

Democrats outspent Republicans on ads ($1.8B vs. $1.4B), but conservative content had far greater *organic* reach—thanks to long-term investment in creators, podcasts, and platforms outside Meta.

**What this means for you:** Start building year-round influencer and content strategies—especially beyond Facebook and Instagram. Create partnerships early and build communities that sustain momentum beyond the campaign window.

---

**🤖 3. AI and Message Testing Are Powerful—But Underused**

AI-powered tools helped generate persuasive content and saved time, and self-service message testing became widely available. Yet both are still mostly experimental.

**What this means for you:** Start treating AI and testing tools as *core campaign infrastructure*. Use them early and often—to shape strategy, not just refine outputs. Don't wait until the final weeks.

---

**🧩 4. Infrastructure Is Strong—but Fragmented**

Progressives have robust data security and voter contact tools—but late tech adoption, inconsistent CRM reliability, and fragmented practices continue to limit impact.

**What this means for you:** Audit your tech stack early. Integrate tools across teams. Push for flexibility, redundancy, and interoperability. Don’t let inertia constrain your strategy.
