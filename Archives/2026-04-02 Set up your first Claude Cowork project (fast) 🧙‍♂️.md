## A wizard prompt that interviews you about your work and builds your entire Cowork project: folders, skills files, and project instructions.

![](https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/c2f9a5f2-0a09-4e2b-b828-42fa6db16309/image.png?t=1747845790)

Hello everybody, welcome back to Moodboard 🌴

[Last week](https://moodboard.beehiiv.com/p/my-guide-to-claude-cowork), I walked through how I set up my Cowork projects: the folder hierarchy, skills files, scheduled tasks, and the overall structure that makes every task compound over time.

But setting up the folder structure, writing your first skills files, figuring out what your project instructions should say... there's a lot of decisions to make before you even start using the thing.

So I built a 901-word ‘wizard prompt’ that guides you through the whole process.

You paste it into a new Cowork project, answer a few questions about your work, and it builds out your entire project setup. Folders, skills files, project instructions, all of it.

Let's get into it 👇

## The Prompt 🧙♂️

If you've been reading Moodboard for a while, you know I'm a fan of wizard prompts (prompts that interview you before building anything). I used the same approach here.

This one clocks in at over 900 words words, so I put it in a Notion doc to keep things organized 😁

Copy/paste it into a new Claude Cowork project and the wizard takes over from there.

## How It Works

The wizard runs through four phases, one question at a time 👇

**Phase 1: Understand your project.** It asks about what you're building, who it's for, and what the repeatable process looks like. If you say "I write a weekly newsletter about AI tools for marketers," it already knows your deliverable, cadence, and audience. It won't re-ask what it can infer (though you can provide as much detail as you want throughout the process).

![](https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/9196d1ea-fbc7-42a3-9722-273e63a40dea/CleanShot_2026-04-01_at_17.44.36.gif?t=1775090693)

**Phase 2: Resources and context.** It figures out what reference materials should live in your project (brand guides, past examples, voice docs, templates, whatever applies) and whether you have files ready to drop in or just need the folder structure built.

**Phase 3: Repetitive workflows.** This is where it identifies which tasks should become skills files. If you do the same type of work every week (writing reports, drafting content, building presentations), the wizard turns those into dedicated skills that Claude can follow every time.

**Phase 4: Build.** Once it has what it needs, it creates everything in one go. Your folder structure, your skills files, and a set of project instructions you can paste directly into your Cowork project settings.

The whole thing takes about five minutes.

## Tips for Getting the Most Out of It

**Have a real project in mind going into it.** The wizard works best when you come in knowing what you want to use Cowork for. "I want to automate my weekly client reports" gives it way more to work with than "I want to try Cowork."

**Start with one project, then expand.** If you have five things you could set up, pick the one you do most often. Get that dialed in, let the skills compound with feedback over a few weeks, and then duplicate the pattern for your other projects.

**Update your skills files as you go.** The setup wizard gives you a great starting point, but the real magic is in the iteration. After Claude runs a task, tell it what to fix and have it write that feedback directly into the skill file. That's how every round of work gets better than the last.

Even if you already have a first project set up, running the wizard for a new one is a good way to pressure-test your structure 🤝
