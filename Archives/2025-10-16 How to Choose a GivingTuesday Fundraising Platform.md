GivingTuesday, which started in 2012, is a global [giving day](https://www.causevox.com/use-case-giving-days/) that inspires millions to support causes they care about. In 2024 alone, GivingTuesday raised $3.6 billion in the U.S., highlighting just how impactful this one day can be for nonprofits. It’s a chance to connect with supporters, reach new audiences, and make a big difference—all within 24 hours.

With this year’s GivingTuesday [quickly approaching on Dec 2, 2025](https://www.givingtuesday.org/), choosing the right fundraising platform is more important than ever. The platform you use affects how easy it is for donors to give, how smoothly you can manage the campaign, and how successful your efforts will be.

In this article, we’ll cover the must-have features to look for your GivingTuesday fundraising platform, so you’re set up for success.

## Ease of Use

Choosing a GivingTuesday fundraising platform that’s easy for both your team and your donors to use will make your campaign run smoothly and encourage more people to give. Here are some key features that make a platform user-friendly:

### Donor Experience

The donor’s journey is at the heart of any successful GivingTuesday campaign. If the donation process is confusing, even the most motivated donor might give up. A few features that can make a big difference include:

- **Seamless Donation Flow:** The [donation process](https://www.causevox.com/blog/online-donation-form/) should be straightforward with minimal steps, reducing friction and allowing donors to complete their gifts quickly.
- **Clear Calls-to-Action (CTAs):** Strong, visually appealing CTAs guide users to take action. Well-designed CTAs can be the difference between a completed donation and someone leaving the page.

### Staff’s Ability to Navigate The Platform

Your team’s [experience with the platform](https://www.causevox.com/blog/how-this-h2go-challenge-raised-over-200k/) is just as important as the donor’s. If the backend is overly complex, it can make it difficult to make quick adjustments, send updates, or keep everything running smoothly. A user-friendly interface lets your staff handle tasks with ease and pivot quickly to any changes.

## Mobile Friendliness: Meeting Your Donors Where They Are

With over [50% of online donations coming from mobile devices](https://mrbenchmarks.com/charts/website-performance), a mobile-friendly platform isn’t just nice to have, it’s essential for GivingTuesday. Your donors should have an effortless experience, no matter what device they’re using. Here are the features to keep in mind:

### Mobile Optimization

Your GivingTuesday campaign needs to look great and work seamlessly on every device. A [platform](https://www.causevox.com/use-case-donation-landing-pages/) that automatically adjusts donation pages for different screen sizes—whether it’s a smartphone, tablet, or desktop—will ensure that your content is always accessible and easy to interact with. But it’s not just about resizing. [Your branding](https://www.causevox.com/blog/stand-out-nonprofit-branding/) should stay intact, with consistent fonts, images, and messaging, so donors feel confident they’re in the right place. Features like fast loading, intuitive navigation, and clear calls-to-action are crucial for keeping users engaged and driving them to complete their donations.

### Mobile Wallets

Convenience is everything when it comes to donations. Platforms that support [mobile wallets](https://www.causevox.com/blog/mobile-wallets-and-nonprofit-fundraising/) like Apple Pay, Google Pay, and Venmo make it easy for donors to give with just a few taps. This quick process reduces the chance of drop-offs and encourages more completed donations. [Venmo](https://www.causevox.com/blog/product-update-ach-venmo-donations/), in particular, can appeal to younger audiences who already use it frequently, helping you reach and engage new supporters more effectively.

Another crucial part of customization is your donation form. A well-optimized form can be the deciding factor between someone completing their donation or dropping off halfway through. Here are a few key things to include:

- **Suggested Donation Amounts:** Providing [suggested gift amounts](https://www.causevox.com/blog/donation-tiers/) helps donors decide quickly on how much to give, and they’re often inspired to choose a slightly higher amount when prompted.
- **Recurring Donation Options:** Always make [recurring gifts](https://www.causevox.com/blog/the-complete-guide-to-recurring-donations/) visible as an option. Many people want to support causes long-term, and simply highlighting this option can convert one-time donors into lifelong supporters.
- **Minimal Fields:** Only ask for what you absolutely need. The more fields someone has to fill out, the more likely they are to abandon the process.

## Reporting and Analytics: Understanding Your Impact

Data is key to understanding how well your campaign is performing and where you can make improvements. Make sure your platform offers comprehensive reporting features.

### Access to Donor Data Throughout and After the Campaign

The ability to access donor data in real time, as well as after the campaign, ensures you can follow up promptly, thank donors, and even plan next year’s campaign more effectively.

### Running Specific Reports

Your platform should make it easy to run [specific reports](https://www.causevox.com/blog/7-fundraising-reports-nonprofit-utilize/) on donations, matching gifts, donor demographics, and more. Having access to detailed reporting helps you measure your campaign’s success in meaningful ways. For example, you could look at the average donation size to determine the effectiveness of your suggested giving levels, or generate a report on donor acquisition to see how well your campaign attracted new supporters. You could also analyze matching gift data to identify which matching opportunities had the greatest impact. These insights are crucial not only for assessing your GivingTuesday performance but also for refining your approach for future fundraising events, allowing you to improve your outcomes.

### CRM Integration

A successful GivingTuesday campaign isn’t just about raising funds, it’s about what happens after the donation. The right platform should help you capture supporter information, follow up meaningfully, and turn one-time givers into lifelong advocates.

If your platform doesn’t connect smoothly with your [CRM](https://www.causevox.com/blog/the-complete-guide-to-nonprofit-crms/), managing this process can get complicated. In that case, having a [built-in CRM](https://www.causevox.com/crm/) that supports your fundraising workflow is key.

What this looks like in practice:

### Unified Donor Profiles

Every donation, event registration, and peer-to-peer gift is automatically linked, giving you a complete view of each supporter. That means when you look up a donor, you can see their entire history with your organization in one place. This holistic view helps your team personalize outreach and strengthen donor relationships beyond GivingTuesday.

### Real-time Syncing and Deduplication

Instead of spending hours cleaning spreadsheets or merging duplicate contacts, a CRM keeps everything updated as gifts come in. Real-time syncing ensures every donor interaction is reflected instantly. Automated deduplication helps maintain accurate records so your reports and follow-ups are always based on reliable data.

### Segmentation and Tagging

A CRM should let you easily tag and [segment supporters](https://www.causevox.com/blog/donor-segmentation-matters/) (first-time givers, returning donors, peer-to-peer fundraisers, etc) so you can send the right message to the right audience. That level of personalization can make a big difference in your [donor retention](https://www.causevox.com/blog/donor-engagement-retention-playbook/) and relationships.

### Tasks and Stewardship Reminders

Staying organized during and after GivingTuesday can be challenging, especially for small teams. Built-in task management and reminders in your CRM can help ensure no donor falls through the cracks. You can assign follow-ups, set reminders to send thank-yous, or track moves toward a larger gift.

### Email and SMS Communication

Reaching out quickly after a gift is crucial for donor retention. A good CRM should include built-in tools for sending personalized thank yous or updates directly.

### Dashboards and Reporting

GivingTuesday campaigns move fast, and having clear, real-time insight into progress can help you make quick decisions. A CRM’s dashboard should let you monitor donations as they come in, track donor activity, and measure engagement. After the campaign, you can generate reports to evaluate what worked and use those insights to plan your next outreach or appeal.

During GivingTuesday, these features make it easier to thank donors promptly, identify first-time donors, and invite them to stay involved. With organized data and built-in communication tools, your team can spend less time juggling systems and more time deepening connections that turn one-day generosity into long-term support.
