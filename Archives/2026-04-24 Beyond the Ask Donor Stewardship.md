### TEAMS! Newsletter | Stewardship Edition | April 24, 2026

The nonprofit sector is often defined by the "hustle" of acquisition, the constant search for new donors and the next big grant. However, after attending the online ***FreeWill Stewardship Summit***, I was struck by a powerful realization: our most significant growth doesn't come from who we find but from how we treat those who have already found us. The data shared was not just interesting; it was a wake-up call for any leader focused on long-term sustainability.

We are operating in a landscape where traditional fundraising is being upended by a "planning gap." While we celebrate the arrival of a new gift, two-thirds of nonprofits lack a documented stewardship strategy. This lack of intentionality is more than an operational oversight; it is a direct threat to our mission's "North Star."

### Executive Summary

The central thesis of the stewardship landscape seminar is simple: **Retention is the new acquisition.** Recapturing a lapsed donor has only a 2% success rate, making it far more efficient to steward and retain current supporters than to constantly hunt for new ones. True stewardship is more than just a "thank you" note; it is a strategic, often automated, yet deeply human journey that doubles a donor’s lifetime impact through consistent, multi-channel engagement.

### Key Takeaways

- ***Retention is Revenue****:* Increasing your retention rate from 80% to 90% makes a new donor twice as generous over a ten-year period.
- ***The Planning Gap****:* Only 1 in 3 organizations uses a written stewardship plan, leaving most donor relationships to chance.
- ***Print’s Surprising Power****:* Donors spend 39% more time with physical mail than email, making print a high-trust, low-competition channel.
- ***Scale Through Automation****:* Stewardship can be scaled by automating task generation while keeping the actual "touchpoints" (calls, notes) human.
- ***Planned Giving Neglect****:* Over 50% of nonprofits fail to steward planned gift donors differently from cash donors, missing a massive opportunity to build legacies.

### Stewardship Insights: Moving from Transaction to Transformation

***The Math of Loyalty:*** We often focus on the "top of the funnel," but the real financial engine of a nonprofit is the "recapture rate." With the success of winning back lapsed donors at an all-time low of 2%, leadership must shift focus. Consider two organizations: Org A retains 90% of its donors, while Org B retains 80%. Over a decade, Org A’s donors are twice as generous. Stewardship isn't just "being nice"; it’s a fiduciary responsibility.

**Closing the Strategy Gap:** The survey data from FreeWill revealed a startling trend: the smaller the organization, the less likely it is to have a stewardship plan or budget. Among organizations under $100K, 100% have no plan. As leaders, we cannot allow "no budget" to mean "no strategy." Creative "layering" that adds personalized touches to existing workflows can bridge this gap without breaking the bank.

**The "Analog" Advantage in a Digital World:** In an era of 140 emails a day, the mailbox is the new frontier. Donors trust print more than any other marketing channel. A "real-world" scenario: instead of a standard annual report, paperclip a handwritten note to the front. Point the donor to a specific page, where the program they funded is featured. This turns a "so what?" document into a "this was for me" moment.

**Automating the "Tired," Humanizing the "Inspired":** Automation should not replace humans; it should free them. Use your CRM to trigger a task when a gift is made, but let a human write the email or make the call. Build "trigger-based" journeys rather than "calendar-based" ones. For example, when a donor is tagged in the CRM, an automated task is assigned to a board member to make a thank-you call within 48 hours.

**The Silent Supporters: Planned Gift Donors:** We are seeing a meta-trend in which bequests as a share of personal giving are rising, reaching 23.7% in 2025. Yet 53% of nonprofits treat these legacy donors exactly like one-time cash givers. These donors have literally written your organization into their wills; they deserve a unique stewardship "stack" that acknowledges their long-term commitment.

### Putting It Into Action: Your 5-Step Plan

- ***Check Out Your Envelopes****:* Stop using #10 "window" envelopes for stewardship; they look like bills and are immediately off-putting. Switch to A-style or oversized envelopes this week.
- ***The "Post-It" Campaign****:* Take five annual reports from your office, add a personalized Post-It note referencing a specific page relevant to each of five top donors, and mail them tomorrow.
- ***Draft a Welcome Series:*** Create a simple, three-email automated sequence for new donors: Email 1 (Immediate thanks), Email 2 (A success story 5 days later), and Email 3 (Ways to get involved beyond money 10 days later).
- ***Schedule a "Thank-You" Party****:* Dedicate 30 minutes of your next board meeting to a "calling party" where members call mid-level donors just to say thank you, no "ask" allowed.
- ***Identify Your "Triggers"****: Define* one event (like a first-time gift or a planned gift disclosure) that will now automatically trigger a personalized response journey in your CRM.

### Closing Comments

Stewardship is the heartbeat of our mission. It is the bridge between a transaction and a shared legacy. When we move beyond the "ask" and focus on the "relationship," we build a community that can weather any storm.
