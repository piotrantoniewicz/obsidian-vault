[The 2026 M+R Benchmarks report](https://mrbenchmarks.com/?utm_campaign=m-r-benchmarks-16-increase-in-email-revenue-in-2025&utm_medium=referral&utm_source=www.civicshoutnewsletter.com) landed yesterday, and it turns out email is a serious revenue generator on its own.

Revenue up 16%.

Revenue per subscriber up from $1.87 to $2.40.

That’s the topline read, and it’s a big one.

The second read is where 2026 planning actually happens — and three findings deeper in the report are quietly pointing at where the next wave of growth comes from.

**Advocacy turns subscribers into stakeholders**  
Advocacy emails generated a 2.3% click-through rate and a 1.4% response rate in 2025. Fundraising emails landed at 0.59% and 0.05% respectively.

The gap is about commitment. An advocacy action is an easier first yes than a gift, and once someone has said yes once, the next yes gets easier.

That’s why 0.34% of advocacy takers donate on the post-action page. They’ve already decided they’re in, and giving in that moment is the most natural next step.

*The move:* Treat advocacy as a fundraising driver, because it is one!

Build your advocacy calendar alongside your fundraising calendar, and follow every action with the story of what it’s fueling.

Supporters keep giving when they can see their money is making a difference.

**Your list is growing and leaking at the same time**  
The headline on list growth looks healthy: 5% net growth.

Underneath, average unsubscribes were 12%, and bounces were another 4%.

That’s 16% of your list walking away every year. In other words, the average nonprofit had to grow by 20% just to net 5%.

*The move:* Prioritize organic and paid acquisition now while there’s time to warm up new supporters before year-end.

A name added in May has months to get to know you before the first #GivingTuesday email drops.

**Your 2025 one-time donors won’t stay one-time donors forever**  
One-time giving grew 17% in 2025, outpacing monthly giving’s 12% growth.

M+R named the catch during their release webinar: emergency donors are often much harder to retain than sustainers.

The donors who showed up big in 2025 are precisely the ones most likely to lapse in 2026, unless you act on that relationship now.

The email chapter of the Benchmarks points out how. Welcome series emails pulled a 1.6% click-through rate in 2025, nearly three times the 0.59% rate of standard fundraising emails.

Triggered sequences sent at high-engagement moments are what the data says works — and the moment right after a first gift is one of those moments.

Sectors that actively converted 2025’s one-time donors into sustainers posted 40% monthly giving growth.

*The move:* If you haven’t asked your 2025 one-time donors to go monthly yet, this quarter is your last best window.

Build a dedicated sustainer ask tied to the specific cause or moment that moved them to give. Then set up a triggered series so the next wave of one-time donors gets the ask automatically.

**The bottom line**  
The big takeaway from this year’s Benchmarks is simpler than I expected: email isn’t going anywhere.

The channel is growing again, per-subscriber value is up, and the organizations winning are the ones activating their existing lists more deliberately.

Plenty to feel good about today, and plenty to act on tomorrow.
