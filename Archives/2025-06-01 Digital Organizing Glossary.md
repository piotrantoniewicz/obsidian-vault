## I. General terms

Ladder of engagement: The "Ladder of Engagement" is a community organizing framework that envisions a series of increasingly impactful actions you ask your supporters to take.

Supporter journey: The path a person takes from initial awareness of a cause to becoming a deeply engaged and active supporter or leader. Mapping this helps in strategic digital engagement.

Landing page: A standalone web page created specifically for a campaign. It's where a visitor "lands" after clicking on a link in an email, ad, or other digital location.

Conversion rate: The percentage of website visitors or email recipients who complete a desired action, such as signing a petition, making a donation, or signing up for an event.

KPI (Key Performance Indicator): A measurable value that demonstrates how effectively a campaign or organization is achieving key objectives.

TOC (Theory of change): A Theory of Change is a description and illustration of how and why a desired change is expected to happen in a particular context. (e.g. If we can do X, then Y will happen)

Crisitunity - crisis+ opportunity: A strategic term in campaigning when a ‘crisis’ creates an ‘opportunity’ to make a change.

## II. Email

Open rate: The percentage of recipients who open your email. A high open rate indicates your subject lines are compelling, your sender name is recognized, and your email list is healthy.

Click rate / Click-through Rate (CTR): The percentage of recipients who click on a link within your email. A strong click rate means your email content is engaging, your Call to Action (CTA) is clear and prominent, and supporters are motivated to take the next step (e.g., sign a petition, watch a video, visit a landing page).

Action rate: The percentage of people who complete a specific desired action after engaging with your email. Examples include signing a petition, RSVPing for an event.

Donation rate: The percentage of individuals who make a financial contribution.

Bounce rate: The percentage of emails that could not be delivered to the recipient's inbox. A high email bounce rate indicates issues with your email list hygiene (outdated or invalid addresses), which can harm your sender reputation.

Unsubscribe rate: The percentage of recipients who opt out of receiving future emails from your list after opening a particular email or series of emails. A high unsubscribe rate can be a red flag. It might mean you're sending too many emails, the content isn't relevant to your audience, or the messaging isn't resonating.

Spam compliant rate: The percentage of recipients who mark your email as "spam" or "junk mail" in their email client. Internet Service Providers (ISPs) track these complaints closely. This is a critical metric for email deliverability and sender reputation.

Personalization: Tailoring digital content, especially emails, to individual supporters based on their data (name, location, past actions, interests) to make communications more relevant and engaging.

Segmentation: The process of dividing an email list or supporter database into smaller groups (segments) based on shared characteristics (e.g., location, interests, donation history) or email activity (read emails in the last 90 or 180 days) to send more targeted messages.

Target audience: The specific group of people that a digital organizing campaign aims to reach with its messages and actions.

## III. Action Network

Ladder: In Action Network, "Ladders" are a specific and powerful automation tool that allows you to build and manage these engagement pathways.

Example:

- Automated Welcome series: Sending a series of onboarding emails to new subscribers.
- Campaign Follow-ups: Automatically sending different messages based on whether a supporter took a specific action.

Letter campaign / mail bombing: A "Letter Campaign" in Action Network is a specific type of action that allows your supporters to easily send pre-written email to designated targets, typically elected officials or other decision-makers.

Daisy chaining: method of connecting multiple actions together in sequence, one after another, so that supporters can flow through the chain from one action to the next. (e.g. A supporter signs a petition, after signing the page will display another action like sharing the petition on whatsapp with a friend.)

## V. Action & engagement

Call to Action (CTA): The language/text we use to elicit an immediate response or activity from the supporter (e.g., "Sign the petition," "Donate now," "Share this post").

Petition / Open letter: A digital tactic containing a written demand made to an authority, signed by many people online, appealing for an action or change.

Supporter survey: A digital tactic, usually shared via email to gather information from supporters. The goal is to better understand their interests, opinions, demographics, motivations, and preferred ways of engaging or contributing.

Small donor fundraising: The practice of raising money through a large number of small contributions of individuals. This approach reduces dependence on large donors or corporate funding.

Gamification: The application of game-design elements and game principles in non-game contexts to engage users and drive desired behaviors (e.g., badges for completing actions).

Peer-to-peer (P2P) fundraising/organizing: A method where supporters solicit donations or engage new people from their own networks, often using specialized digital tools or platforms.

## VI. Technical terms

API (Application Programming Interface): A set of rules and protocols that allows different software applications to communicate with each other. Essential for integrating various digital tools.

CRM (Customer Relationship Management): Software systems used to manage and analyze customer or, in our case, activist interactions and data throughout the supporter lifecycle. Crucial for tracking engagement and communications.

Geotargeting: Delivering content or ads to a user based on their geographic location. Useful for localized campaigns.

Mobile-First: A design philosophy that prioritizes the mobile user experience when developing websites and digital content, recognizing that a significant portion of online activity occurs on mobile devices.

Analytics: Analytics is the process of collecting, measuring, and analyzing data to discover patterns, trends, and insights that inform decision-making.

Dashboard: A dashboard is a visual tool that collects data and metrics in a clear, organized way, allowing users to monitor, analyze, and make decisions based on real-time data.

A/B Testing: A method of comparing two versions of a webpage, email, ad, or other digital content to see which one performs better. (Also applies to Email Marketing)
